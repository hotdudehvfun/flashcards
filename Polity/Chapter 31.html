Chapter 31 Chief Minister

Part VI deals with State Executive
Governor Head of State
CM head of Government

Article 164: CM shall be appointed by Governor.
Governor appoints leader of majority. then asks him to prove confidence on floor.
He need not to prove confidence before appointed, only after.
also if not a member of assembly/council, must get elected in 6 months

Oath
  1. of allegiance and secrecy, by governor
  2. holds office during pleasure of governor, means can dismiss and dissolve lower but not at his will, only when losing confidence.
  3. salary determined by state legislature/
Powers
  1. Head of Council
    1. governor appoint those, who are recommended by CM
    2. allocates portfolios
    3. can ask for resign or ask governor to dismiss any member
    4. if CM resigns, council collapse
  2. Channel of information between party and governor
  3. Advises appointments of advocate general, SPSC, state election commissioner.
  4. Advises governor to summon/prorogue sessions
  5. Can recommend dissolution of govt.
  6. Announces govt. policies on floor
  7. Others:
    1. Member of Inter-State Council, NDC headed by PM
    2. Chairman of State Planning Board

Constitutional Provision Part VI CM
  1. Article 163: there shall be a council of minister with CM to aid and advise. Interesting to note, like president governor is not bound be advise as amended by 42nd Act 1976.
  2. Article 164:
    1. CM appointed by governor and other ministers appointed by advise of CM
    2. Holds office during pleasure of governor
    3. Collective responsibility of council.
  3. Article 166:
    1. Conduct of business of govt. of state
  4. Article 167
    1.  Duty of CM to furnish such information as such governor may call.
