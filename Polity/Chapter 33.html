Part VI Article 168-212

Organisation of State Legislature
  1. No uniformity at state levels
  2. Only 6 states are bicameral: AP,UP,Bihar, Karnataka, Maharashtra, Telangana, 
  3. Some history
    1. Tamil nadu council act 2010 never came into force
    2. In AP council formed by Legislative Council Act 2005
    3. 7th Act 1956 provided for MP but not enforced
    4. Due to bifurcation J&K, does not have upper house
  4. How to get a Legislative Council?
    1. resolution passed by Legislative Assembly by special majority(present and sitting)
    2. simple majority by parliament, as passed by simple it does not come under Article 368, but still constitution is amended.

Legislative Assembly
  1. Strength:
    1. Assembly: min 60 max 500. varies according to constituencies
    2. Exception: Arunachal Pradesh, Sikkim, Goa: min is 30
    3. Mizoram: 40, Nagaland: 46
  2. Nominated Member: only One from anglo-indian community, originally for 10 years but has been extended again and again
  3. Readjustment:
    1. Delimitation Act:Power of parliament to adjust seats in states after a census
    2. 42nd Act froze seats till 2000 on level of 1971, 84th again froze till 2026 on level of 1991
    3. later 87th act change base year to 2001 census
  4. Reservation:
    1. provision for reserved seats for SCs, STs in each state
    2. originally for 10 years but had been extended continuously
    3. 95th Act extended till 202
  5. Duration:
    1. normal 5 years
    2. governor can dissolve
    3. During National Emergency, assembly period can be extended 1 year at a time.
  6. Qualification:
    1. citizen, age 25
    2. oath under person authorized by election commission
    3. must be an elector for constituency in state, for nomination residence is a requirement
    4. for SC, ST seat must be a member of SC,ST
  7. Disqualifications
    1. office of profit
    2. unsound mind
    3. insolvent
    4. not a citizen any more
    5. disqualified under law made by parliament
    6. must not guilty under offences or corrupt
    7. should not be convicted for offence in imprisonment for two or more years
    8. but preventive detention law does not amount to disqualification.
    9. must not have failed to lodge an account of his election expenses
    10. dismissal from govt. service
    11. must not have been punished for untouchability, dowry, sati
  8. Disqualification for Defection:
    1. 10th schedule
    2. Final decision by speaker, chairman, but can be review in court.
  9. Oath
    1. has to become a member in 6 months
    2. unless take a oath he cannot vote, participate and does not become eligible to privileges.
    3. fine for 500 each day
  10. How seats are vacated of Seats
    1. Double Membership: if a person, gets seats in both house, he must intimate else seat of one house gets vacated as per provision by legislature.
    2. Disqualification:
    3. Resign to Speaker/Chairman
    4. Absence: 60 days
    5. Others: Election is void, expelled, becomes President, VP, Governor
  11. Speaker:
    1. From Elected members
    2. Removal 14 days notice: resolution passed by effective majority( majority of present)
    3. Powers:
      1. maintains order and decorum
      2. final interpreter of provisions
      3. adjourns/suspend
      4. does not vote in first instance but can cast a tie vote
      5. allowed secret sitting
      6. decides on money bill
      7. disqualifications on defection
      8. appoints chairmen of committees
      9. Chairman of Business Advisory Committee, Rules Committee, general purpose committee.
  12. Deputy speaker of House
    1. elected after election of speaker
    2. presides when speaker is absent
    3. Speaker can also appoints member of chairpersons when speaker and deputy speaker both are absent


Legislative Council
  1. Strength
    1. min: 40 max: 1/3rd of assembly
  2. Manner of Election ( S T V ): Let's say total seats=12
    1. 4 members: local bodies
    2. 1 member : by graduates of 3 years
    3. 1 member: teachers of 3 year, not lower than secondary school
    4. 4 members from assembly who are not current members: by members of assembly
    5. 2: nominated by governor
    6. Total 10 elected 5/6th and 1/6th nominated
    7. Parliament can change above scheme.
  3. Duration: Continuing body, 1/3rd member retire every 2nd year.
  4. Chairman:
    1. from members of council
    2. resign to deputy chairman
    3. can be removed by resolution after giving 14 days notice
  5. Deputy Chairman



STATE LEGISLATURE
  1. Summoning:
    1. by governor, more than 6 month gap
  2. Adjournment:
    1. Suspends the house for next meeting
    2. Can be sine die means indefinite period
  3. Prorogation:
    1. End of a session
  4. Dissolution: Bill Lapse Rules
    1. Lapse when:
      1. pending in lower house whether origin or sent by upper house( because next govt. does not want policies of previous govt.)
      2. passed by lok sabha but pending in rajya sabha, again next govt. would not be happy with bills passed by lower house.
    2. No Lapse:
      1. bill origin in upper house and pending 
      2. reserved for governor or president
  5. Quorum: 10% of total including presiding officer
  6. Voting:
    1. normally by simple majority: present and voting
    2. removal of speaker, deputy speaker, or any other constitutional provision requires special majority.
  7. Language:
    1. english or hindi
    2. earlier english was to be allowed for 15 years
  8. Rights of Ministers:
    1. can take part in proceedings any of house but can vote only in parent house
    2. a minister who is not a member of house is allowed in proceedings
  9. Ordinary Bills
    1. in any house
    2. goes through 5 stages: 
      1. first : intro and title
      2. second: detailed discussion handles by committee
      3. third: voting final reject and accept
      4. fourth: same in other house
      5. fifth: assent of governor or president
    3. Assembly: can override council by sending bill again, hence no deadlock in state legislation
      1. bill passed by assembly sent to council
      2. now council may reject bill all together, or make changes or may pass as it is.
      3. if assembly accepts the changes well and good.
      4. but if assembly does not accept changes, and council does not take any action for 3 three months. then assembly can sent bill again.
      5. even if this time, council  does not take action in 1 month, then bill is deemed to be passed as passed by assembly second.
      6. if bill passed by council sent to assembly, and assembly ejects it becomes dead
    4. Assent of Governor:
      1. assent, withhold, return, reserve for president
      2. if returned and pass again then has to give assent
      3. when reserved for president, he can assent, withhold, return, if again passed with in 6 month, president can take any action.
  10. Money Bills
    1. only in lower house requires prior permission of governor
    2. only by minister
    3. council can only make recommendations within 14 days
    4. governor can assent, withhold, but no return, same if reserved for president
Position of Council a white elephant🐘
  1. Equal to Assembly:
    1. only passing of ordinary bills but overridable by assembly if council disagree
    2. ordinances passed by governor
    3. cm can be selected from council
    4. consideration of reports
    5. enlargement of jurisdiction of SPSC
  2. UnEqual Assembly: much weaker than Rajya Sabha
    1. money bill
    2. final passing of ordinary bills
    3. no control over grants
    4. not cut motions
    5. election of president
    6. existence of council can be ended by assembly
    7. ratification of constitutional amendment

Privileges
  1. Does not extended to governor
  2. Collective
    1. publish reports
    2. exclude strangers from meetings
    3. rules regarding procedures
    4. punish for contempt
    5. right to receive information for arrest
    6. courts cannot inquire into proceedings
    7. no person can be arrest or no legal process without permission of presiding officer
  3. Individuals
    1. cannot be arrest before and after of 40 days of a session, only for civil no for criminal cases
    2. no jury service
