Chapter 30 Governor
PART VI deals with state governments

Article 153-167: Executive of State

State Executive: Governor, CM and his council, Advocate General

7th Act: same person for two or more states as governor, the salaries are shared by states decide by president

Appointment of Governor
  1. Appointed by president by warrant under his hand and seal
  2. Post of governor is a independent constitutional post not employment under government.
Draft Constitution proposed election of governor but Constituent Assembly did not opt for that due to following reasons
  1. to avoid conflict between CM and Governor as head of state
  2. office is a nominal head of state so no need to elect him
  3. would not be neutral
  4. elected person may not follow unitary feelings
  5. CM may also influence to get his player as governor to get supreme control over state
Hence American Model idea where Governor is elected directly was not adopted
Canadian model was adopted where governor is appointed by governor-general of centre

Qualifications
  1. citizen
  2. age > 35 years
  3. Only:Conventions
  4. outsider 
  5. president should consult with CM, although above two have been violated many times
Conditions for Office
  1. not a member of state or center parliament
  2. no office of profit
  3. get Raj Bhavan to live
Governor is also immune from criminal proceedings
but giving 2 months notice civil proceedings can be initiated in respect of his personal acts.
Oath
  1. administered by CHIEF JUSTICE OF HIGH COURT any other senior most judge

Tenure
  1. 5 years but holds office until successor comes
  2. pleasure of president
  3. resigns to president
  4. Most important "No grounds mentioned to remove", President can remove them anytime on any ground.
  5. President can also transfer him at will
  6. Moreover, President can make provision as he think fit which are not provided in Constitution.



Powers of Governor
  1. Executive
    1. actions in his name
    2. make rules in which orders are executed
    3. make rules for convenient transaction of state government
    4.  appointed CM and council on his advise, council holds pleasure of governor.
    5. appoints Advocate General, holds during pleasure of governor
    6. appoints State Election Commissioner, also determines his service and tenure, removal same as of high court judge
    7. appoints members of SPSC, but removal by president
    8. recommend president's rule
    9. act as a Chancellor of universities, also appoint vice chancellors
  2. Legislative Powers
    1. Summon/prorogue assembly
    2. can address assembly at start of year and also start of new govt.
    3. sends message regarding pending bills
    4. power to appoint presiding officer when speaker and deputy both unavailable
    5. Nominated 1 Anglo Indian to SLA and 1/6th members to SLC
    6. Question of disqualification with election commission
    7. Governor can assent, withhold, or return normal bills
    8. can also reserve bills of president, after that governor has not roll in bill, also president gets absolute power over bill
    9. Obligatory reserve bills of he thinks passed bill may endangers position of high court.
    10. also if bill is of 
      1. Ultra vires
      2. against DPSP, large interest of country
      3. grave national importance
      4. Article 31-C acquisition of property
    11. Ordinance power
      1. when house not in session
      2. same as president ordinance making power is not discretionary and has to act on advice of council and head of council
      3. after reassembly house can pass resolution to withdraw or continue but in 6 weeks of first meeting
      4. if no action within 6 weeks then ordinance dies
      5. Required president permission in  following cases before Passing Ordinance:
      6. bill which would have require sanction of president
      7. necessary to reserve for president
      8. act which will be invalid without president's assent
    12. lays reports received from finance commission, spsc in front of assembly
  3. Financial Powers
    1. money bills require his prior permission
    2. no grants except on his recommendation
    3. can take advances out of contingency fund
    4. constitutes finance commission 
  4. Judicial Powers
    1. Consult president while appointing high court judges
    2. also make appointment, postings, promotions of district judges in consult with high court
    3. appoints persons to judicial service
    4. Pardoning Powers:
      1. pardon, reprieve, respite, remit, suspend, commute in case of state law only
      2. cannot pardon death sentence, only can suspend, remit or commute, only president can pardon death sentences
      3. president can pardon even in cases of court-martial but governor cannot 

Constitutional Provisions
  1. Article 154: executive powers of governor
  2. Article 163: there shall be a council of minister headed by CM to aid Governor
  3. Article 164: collective responsibility of council
  4. Difference between discretionary powers of President and Governor
    1. Advice of council is binding on president as amended by 42nd Act 1976
    2. But no such provision for Governor, hence discretionary power of governor is greater than president
  5. Governor can act on his own in following ways based on Constitutional provisions
    1. reserve bills of president
    2. recommend president's rule
    3. Royalty accruing from licenses for mineral exploration
    4. seeking information from CM
  6. Situational Discretion of Governor
    1. appoints CM when no party, on obvious successor
    2. dismiss council when lost confidence
    3. dissolve SLA on losing majority
