High Courts Article 214-231

High Courts below SC and above subordinate courts
1862: origin of High Courts in Calcutta, Bombay, Madras
1866: Allahabad
1950: HC of province was made HC of State

Provision of having HCs in each state.
7th Act 1956: two states can have same courts
Current: 24 HCs, 4 are common
Parliament can exclude or include jurisdiction of a HC

Organisation of HC
  1. Constitution did not specify strength, discretion of president
  2. CJI and other judges
  3. Appointment of HC Judges:President
    1. Chief Justice by president after consulting CJI and governor
    2. Judges by president after consulting chief justice.
    3. Second Judge Case 1993: to appoint high court president consult CJI
    4. Third Judge Case 1998: SC said in addition to consult CJI, CJI must consult collegium of Two Senior most judges of SC to appoint HC judges. and 4 in case of SC Judges
    5. Fourth Judge Case 2015: 99th Act replace collegium by NJAC. SC made it void. Hence collegium system is at present
  4. Qualifications for Judge
    1. citizen
    2. 10 year practice in HCs
    3. no minimum age
    4. NOTE: unlike SC judge, distinguished jurist is not mention for HC judge.
  5. Oath:
    1. By Governor
  6. Tenure:
    1. Not fixed
    2. till age of 62, of SC judge age is 65
    3. questions regarding age is decided by president after consulting CJI
    4. resigns to president
  7. Removal: Same as of SC judge(motion,committee,address)
    1. Removal motion signed by 100 if rises in lok sabha and 50 if charges are raised in Rajya Sabha.
    2. Speaker may or may not admit motion
    3. After that, 3 member committee investigates(CJI/SC judge, CJ of HC, 1 Jursit)
    4. then motion has to be passed by special majority in both houses
    5. Judge is removed.
  8. Transfer:
    1. President can transfer
    2. Transfer can also be review by SC but only judge can challenge it,
    3. Third Judge Case 1998: SC said CJI , 4 judges of SC and 2 CJ of HCs one from which he is being transferred and second to which is to be transferred.
  9. Acting Chief Justice
    1. by president:
    2. office is vacant
    3. temporarily absent
    4. unable to perform duties.
  10. Additional Judges
    1. by president for no more than 2 years
  11. Retired Judges
    1. CJ of HC, can request retired judge with consent of president and that person to temporarily act as a HC judge.
Independence of HC
  1. Mode of appointment
  2. Security of Tenure
  3. fixed service conditions
  4. expenses charged on CFI
  5. conduct cannot be discussed
  6. ban on practice after retirement
  7. punish for its contempt
  8. free to appoint staff
  9. jurisdiction cannot be curtailed

Powers of HC
  1. Original Jurisdiction
    1. Admiralty, will, marriage, divorce, company, contempt of court
    2. election disputes of parliament and state legislature.
    3. revenue matters
    4. FRs
    5. cases of subordinate courts involving interpretation of constitution
  2. Writs
    1. Article 226: for FRs and other laws
    2. for any other legal right
    3. to person, govt.   or authority outside of jurisdiction also
    4. Chandra Kumar Case 1997: SC and HC writ powers are basic structure and cannot be take away.
  3. Appellate Jurisdiction
    1. to hear disputes by way of appeal
    2. Civil Matters
      1. appeals from orders of subordinate courts
      2. on both question of law and facts
      3. Calcutta, Bombay, Madras high court have provision of inter court appeals
      4. appeals from decisions of administrative and other tribunals.
    3. Criminal matters
      1. appeals from sessions court
      2. if punishment is more than 7 years
      3. if capital punishment given by session court,  has to be confirmed by HC whether person appeals or not.
  4. Supervisory Jurisdiction
    1. superintendence over courts and tribunals except military
    2. prescribe rules, books
    3. settle fees payable to clerks, sheriffs
    4. this power is broad and extended to all courts and tribunals
    5. however this is not an unlimited power can should be exercised in gross violation, error of law, excess of jurisdiction. 
  5. Control over Subordinate Courts
    1. Administrative controls
    2. Consult by governor while appointing district court judges
    3. HC deals with disciplinary actions of judicial service of state other than district judges
    4. Can withdraw a pending case
    5. Law binding on all subordinate courts
  6. A Court of Record
    1. Contempt of Court not defined in constitution
    2. Only in Contempt of Court Act 1971
    3. Contempt can be Civil(willful disobedience) or Criminal(scandal, interfere in proceedings)
    4. Reviewing power is not conferred on HC by constitution, as in case of SC
  7. Judicial Review
    1. can review centre and state orders
    2. although judicial review is no where defined in case of 
      1. FRs
      2. outside the competence of authority 
      3. against constitutional values
      4. 42nd Act: HC cannot review
      5. 43rd Act: HC can review
