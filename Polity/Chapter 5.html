Union and its Territory
 

Article 1
  1. India, that is, Bharat as a Union of States rather than a Federation of States
  2. India, Bharat: two names
  3. Union of States: shows  Polity
  4. Indian Federation is not the result of an agreement among the states like the America
  5. States have no right to secede from the federation in India
  6. states have been divided only for administrative purpose
According to Article 1
  1. Territories of states
  2. Union Territories
  3. Territories that may acquire
Territory of India: states but also union territories and territories that may be acquired by the Government of India at any future time
Union of India: includes only states
Hence territory of india is wider than union of India

Article 2
  1. Admission(only new ones, which are not part of India)
  2. Admit already established states
  3. Establish  New states
  4. above states are not part of India
Article 3: Three: Alteration(Internal Readjustment)
  1. power to merge two states or split
  2. increase/decrease area or boundary
  3. Change Name of States
  4. Rules
    1. Bill to alter required prior permission of president
    2. President has to refer same to concerning state
    3. views of states are non binding on president or parliament
    4. also in case of UTs, parliament can itself take action.
  5. parliament can redraw map at its will
  6. this shows territorial integrity of any state is not guaranteed by the constitution
  7. USA: federals  can secede from State.

Article 4
  1. Amendments for Article 2 and Article 3 are not constitutional amendments according to Article 368.
  2. hence only simple majority is required.

but this was challenged in SC

1960 President made reference to SC under Article 143 to examine Article 3
Berubari Case 1960.: a case related to thana
SC said parliament cannot cede territory without amending the constitution.
Parliament enacted 9th Act 1960 to transfer territory to pakistan.

1969: SC said settlement of boundary dispute does not require amending constitution and can be done by action of executive as it does not involve cession of territory.

100th Act 2015: to transfer 3 enclaves to Bangladesh, while bangladesh transfer 51 to India




Evolution of States and UTs
Princely States
  1. Two categories: British provinces and Princely States
  2. Independence Act 1947: created Ind and Pak and give options to princely states to join, either or none.
  3. out of 552 449 joined but Hyd, Junagarh, Kashmir refused.
  4. Hyd by police action, Junagarh by referendum, Kashmir by Accession
  5. 1950: States in four Parts: A,B,C,D
  6. A: governor's provinces
  7. B: princely states
  8. C: chief commissioner's
  9. D: Andaman and Nicobar islands 
1948: Dhar Commission
  1. to examine the feasibility of reorganisation on basis of linguistics
  2. Recommended reorganisation on basis of administrative convenience rather than linguistic factor.
  3. created resentment and led to fresh commission
1948: JVP Committee
  1. Rejected reorganisation of states on language basis
1953: Andhra Pradesh first state based on language
by separating the Telugu speaking areas from the Madras state.     

1953: Fazl Ali Commission
  1. Reexamine reorganisation question again
  2. Members: fazl, panikkar, Kunzru
  3. broadly accepted language as basis
  4. Rejected one language one state theory
  5. Four factors while reorganizing
    1. Preservation of Unity and Security
    2. Linguistic and cultural homogeneity
    3. Financial, Economic and administrative considerations
    4. Welfare of the people
  6. Abolition of the four-fold classification (A,B,C,D)
  7. accepted by govt.

States Reorganisation Act 1956 
  1. four fold categories was removed
  2. 14 states and 6 UTs
  3. UTs in 1956:
    1. Andaman and Nicobar Islands
    2. Delhi
    3. Himachal Pradesh
    4. Laccadive Minicoy and Amindivi Islands: from detached madras state.
    5. Manipur

    1. Kerala: merged travancore-cochin with madras

    4. Bombay: saurashtra and kutch
