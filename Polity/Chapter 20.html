Chapter 20 Council of Ministers
Constitutional position of council
Article 74 :
1. council headed by pm to aid and advise president
2. advice cannot be inquired into any court

Article 75 :other provisions as to ministers 
  1. pm appointed by president and president appoint only those recommended by pm
  2. council cannot be greater than 15% of total strength
  3. collective responsibility of ministers, when lok sabha dissolves, members of raj sabha also have to resign
  4. oath under president
Article 77
  1. All executive action of the Government of India shall be expressed to be taken in the name of the President
  2.  President shall make rules for the more convenient transaction of the business of the Government of India
Article 78
  1. duties of pm
Article 88
  1. ministers can take part in either of house, joint sitting but not entitled to vote


Nature of advice by council
  1. binding on president by 42nd and 44th act
  2. cannot be inquired in any court
  3. president cannot exercise without council even when lok sabha is dissolved. they holds office until next council is formed
  4. satisfaction is not of president but of council
  5. salaries and other allowances of council determined by parliament

No Legal Responsibility 
  1. no legal responsibility in india
  2. but in britain ministers counter signed the passed laws
Composition Council
  1. three types
  2. Cabinet, Ministers of State, Deputy Ministers
  3. ministers of state are attached with cabinet ranks
  4. deputies assists in work of cabinets

COUNCIL OF MINISTERS VS CABINET 
  1. council:60 to 70 ministers, cabinet:15 to 20
  2. all types, only cabinet ranks
  3. does not meet as a body, cabinet meet as a collective body
  4. power in theory, all powers exercise by cabinet
  5. council article 74, 75, cabinet inserted in article 352 by 44th
