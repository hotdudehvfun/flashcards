Chapter 27 Judicial Review

Original and developed in USA
Judicial review is not explicitly mention in constitution
But following articles:
Article 13: laws inconsistent with or in derogation of FRs

Article 32:
Article 226:writs power of HC for FRs and other laws

Article 131:Original jurisdiction of SC in centre-state disputes
Article 132: Appellate jurisdiction(constitutional matters)
Article 133: Appellate jurisdiction(civil matters)
Article 134: Appellate jurisdiction(criminal matters)
Article 134-A: HC certify appeal to SC
Article 135: SC can act on pre-constitutional laws
Article 136: SC grant for special leave

Article 143: President can take advice on matters by SC, of public importance(SC may refuse), pre-constitutional(SC cannot refuse)

Article 227: HC is above district and other low level courts

Article 372: continuance of force of pre-constitution laws.


Scope of Judicial Review
  1. against FRs
  2. outside competence of authority
  3. against constitutional values
NINTH SCHEDULE
  1. Article 31-B: save laws form judicial reviews by placing them in 9th schedule
  2. Added by 1st act 1951
  3. I.R Coehlho 2007 case: Sc ruled laws under Ninth schedule are up for judicial review
  4. it said laws added after 1973, can be reviewed if violated Article 14,15,19,21
