Chapter 16 Emergency Provisions (Article 352,356,360,365,358,359)


National Emergency art 352
1. During war, external or armed rebellion
2. Whole or part of India
3. 44th 1978 :internal disturbance out armed rebellion in
4. President only after receiving a written recommendation from the cabinet. 
5. 44th 1978: Only on the Concurrence of the cabinet and not merely on the advice of the prime minister 
6. 38th 1975 :No judicial review but 44th amendment yes judicial review. Minerva Mills case (1980) provision can be challenged in court
7. Approval and Duration
1. Resolution:  Both House in ONE MONTH by SPECIAL MAJORITY same procedure to continue after 6 month
2. Life of proclamation: 6 MONTH ONLY. After that must be approved again. Cannot proclaim for indefinite period.
3. If lower house gets dissolve during that SIX MONTH period. And further resolution has not been passed, proclamation can survive only for 30 days since last meeting provided passed by Upper House.
8. Revoke Proclamation?
1. President own its own with approval of parliament
2. Has to revoke by resolution passed by Parliament
3. If 10% of total members of Lok Sabha i:e at least 55 give notice to speaker or president (when not in session), a joint sitting must be held within 14 days 
4. Simple Majority only Lok Sabha is sufficient
9. Continuation
a. same as to start new proclamation: Special Majority both HOUSES
10. Effects of National Emergency
1. Complete control of the Centre, though state are not suspended
2. Make laws on any subject mentioned in the State List, legislative power of a state legislature is not suspended, laws lifetime 6 months after emergency ends
3. President can issue ordinances if parliament is not in session
4. Parliament can confer powers and impose duties upon the Centre
5. 42nd Amendment 1976: extends not only to a state where the Emergency is in operation but also to any other state
6. President can either reduce or cancel the transfer of finances from Centre to the states
7. Life of Lok Sabha can be extended beyond 5 years, 1 year at a time. Can be used any number of times. But ceased to exist after 6 months of end of emergency. Also applicable to state government.
11. Effects on FR
1. Art 358
a. AUTOMATICALLY Suspends art 19
b. But requires External Rebellion or WAR
c. Only laws related to emergency cannot be challenged in court but other laws which affected art 19 can be challenged after emergency

2. Art 359
a. Suspend right to seek remedy but rights are not suspended
b. Works in both external or internal rebellion
c. Requires approval from both the houses
d. 20 and 21 cannot be suspended

3. provide immunity from challenge to only those laws which are related with the Emergency and not other laws

12. So far Three Times
1. 1962-1968 aggression in NEFA
2. 1971 Bangladesh War
3. 1975 internal rebellion

13. Shah Commission found emergency unjustified
1. did not justify the declaration of the Emergency
2. hence 44th amendment act 1978




B. State Emergency art 356 also known as President's Rule
1. Art 355 duty of centre that govt. of state runs in accordance of constitution

2.Art 356 president satisfied state cannot be carried on in accordance of the Constitution
a. Can act on a report of governor of the state or otherwise too

3.Art 365 if state failed to comply

4.Approval and Duration
1. First time, by Both houses in TWO MONTHS SIMPLE MAJORITY
2. Max duration SIX MONTHS , Max period THREE YEARS, can be extended SIX MONTHS at a time by approval of parliament
3. Extension: SIMPLE MAJORITY ANY HOUSE

5. Extension: after ONE YEAR CONDITION
a. proclamation of National Emergency should be in operation in the whole of India, or in the whole or any part of the state; and
b. Election Commission must certify that the general elections to the legislative assembly of the concerned state cannot be held on account of difficulties.

6. REVOKE: president without parliament approval

7. Effect on STATE
1. President take up functions
2. Can declare powers of state legislature to be exercised by parliament
3. Dismisses state council headed by CM and governor performs duties with help of chief  secretary appointed by President
4.     President can dissolve or suspends legislative assembly
a. Parliament can delegate the power to make laws for the state to the President or to any other authority
b. President can authorize, when the Lok Sabha is not in session, expenditure from the state consolidated fund pending its sanction by the Parliament
c. President can promulgate, when the Parliament is not in session, ordinances for the governance of the state.
5. Laws made during rule can continue in operation unless repealed or altered.
6. State High Court continues to function.


Bommai case 1994 regarding state emergency
a. Subject to judicial review
b. Satisfaction of the President must be based on relevant material, can be struck down if it was found to be malafide or perverse
c. Burden lies on Centre
d. Court cannot go into correctness of the material
e. Court can restore the dismissed state government if found unconstitutional proclamation
f. State legislative assembly should be dissolved only after Parliament approved the presidential proclamation, until such approval is given,president can only suspend the assembly
g. In case the Parliament fails to approve the proclamation, the assembly would get reactivated
h. Secularism is 'basic features' 
i. The question of the state government losing the confidence of the legislative assembly should be decided on the floor of the House and until that is done the ministry should not be unseated
j. Where a new political party assumes power at the Centre, it will not have the authority to dismiss ministries formed by other parties in the states
k. Article 356 is an exceptional power


8. PROPER USE of Article 356
a. Hung Assembly
b. No majority no coalition ministry
c. No willing party
d. Internal disturbance leads to internal subversion
e. Breakdown of govt.



C. Finance Emergency art 360
1. Financial stability is threatened
2. Satisfaction of President is up for Judicial Review
3. Approval and Duration
1. Both houses within TWO MONTHS by SIMPLE MAJORITY
2. If Lok Sabha gets dissolved within that 2 month period time, proclamation survives only 30 days from last sitting
3. No MAX LIMIT
4. Continuation does not require parliament  approval again
4. Revoke
1. President on its own with parliament approval
