PROCEDURE FOR AMENDMENT 
Article 368 
1.Only by Bill ,in Either House of Parliament and not State Legislatures.
2. Minister or by Private member, Does not require prior permission of the president
3.Special Majority, 
4. Houses must pass the bill separately. No Joint Sitting 
5. Federal provisions Ratified :half of the states by a simple majority,
6. After passed by both houses and state legislatures, Bill is presented to the president for Assent.
7. President must give his assent to the bill. NO RETURN, NO WITHHOLD 
8. Only After President’s Assent, the Bill Becomes an Act (i.e., a constitutional amendment act) and the Constitution stands amended in accordance with the terms of the Act.

TYPES OF AMENDMENTS AVAILABLE
1. Simple Majority(does not comes under article 368)
2. Special Majority
3. Special Majority + Ratification of half of states
Article 368 only two methods
1. Only by Special Majority
2. Special + Ratification
Simple majority does not fall under Article 368 this means most of the amendments can be done without changing text of constituion

CRITICISM OF THE AMENDMENT PROCEDURE 
1. There is no provision for a special body like Constitutional Convention (as in USA) or Constitutional Assembly for amending the Constitution. The constituent power is vested in the Parliament and only in few cases, in the state legislatures

2. Only parliament can start amendment except in one case, that is, passing a resolution requesting the Parliament for the creation or abolition of legislative councils in the states. Here also, the Parliament can either approve or disapprove such a resolution or may not take any action on it

3. Major part of the Constitution can be amended by the Parliament alone either by a special majority or by a simple majority. Only in few cases, the consent of the state legislatures is required and that too, only half of them, while in USA, it is three-fourths of the states.

4. The Constitution does not prescribe the time frame within which the state legislatures should ratify or reject an amendment submitted to them. Also, it is silent on the issue whether the states can withdraw their approval after according the same. 

5. No provision for holding a joint sitting 

6. The process of amendment is similar to that of a legislative process. Except for the special majority, the constitutional amendment bills are to be passed by the Parliament in the same way as ordinary bills

7. The provisions relating to the amendment procedure are too sketchy. Hence, they leave a wide scope for taking the matters to the judiciary.
