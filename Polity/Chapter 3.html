Features of Constitution


Kesavananda Bharati case (1973)
the Supreme Court ruled that the constituent power of Parliament under Article 368 does not enable it to alter the ëbasic structureí of the Constitution.

  1. Lengthiest Written Constitution
    1. 1949, 1 Preamble, 395 Articles (divided into 22 Parts) and 8 Schedule
    2. Large size due to
      1. Geographical area
      2. influence of the Government of India Act of 1935
      3. Single Constitution for both the Centre and the states except Jammu and Kashmir
      4. Dominance of legal personalities in the Constituent Assembly
  2. Various Sources
    1. Government of India Act of 1935
      1. Federal Scheme
      2. Office of Governor
      3. Judiciary
      4. Public Service Commissions
      5. Emergency provisions
    2. British Constitution
      1. . Parliamentary government
      2. . Rule of Law
      3. . Legislative Procedure
      4. . Single citizenship
      5. . Cabinet system
      6. . Prerogative writs
      7. . Bicameralism
    3. US Constitution
      1. . Fundamental rights
      2. . Independence of Judiciary
      3. . Judicial review
      4. . Impeachment of the president
      5. . Removal of SC and HC judges
      6. . Vice-president Post
    4. Irish Constitution
      1. . Directive Principles
      2. . Nomination of members to Rajya Sabha
      3. . Method of election of president
    5. Canadian Constitution
      1. . Strong Centre
      2. . Residuary powers in the Centre
      3. . Appointment of State Governors by the Centre
      4. . Advisory jurisdiction of the Supreme Court
    6. Australian Constitution
      1. . Concurrent List
      2. . Freedom of Trade, Commerce and Intercourse
      3. . Joint sitting of the Two Houses
    7. Weimar Constitution of Germany
      1. . Suspension of Fundamental Rights during Emergency
    8. Soviet Constitution USSR
      1. . Fundamental Duties
      2. . Ideal of justice (Social Economic Political) in the Preamble
    9. French Constitution
      1. . Republic and Liberty, Equality, Fraternity in the Preamble
    10. South African Constitution
      1. . Procedure for amendment of the Constitution and election of members of Rajya Sabha
    11. Japanese Constitution
      1. .Procedure established by Law
  3. Rigid and Flexible Constitution
    1. Article 368
      1. Special majority of the Parliament
      2. Special Majority with the Ratification by Half of the Total States
    2. Simple Majority: for ordinary legislative process, constitution can be amended but not all provision, this does not fall under Article 368
  4. Federal Features of Constitution
    1. .Centre and State Government: Two Government system
    2. .Powers of state defined
    3. .Written Constitution guarantees existence of centre and state
    4. .Supremacy of Constitution
    5. .Bicameralism
    6. .Independent Judiciary: does not affected by centre government
  5. Unitary Features
    1. Centre can suspend state rule in emergency by simple majority
    2. .Governor appointed by Centre
    3. .Single Constitution
    4. .All India Services
    5. .Integrated Judiciary :SC is on top of all courts
  6. Indian is Quasi-Federal
    1. .It is Union of States Article 1
    2. .States cannot secedes from Union
  7. Parliamentary Government Features
    1. Nominal and Real Heads
    2. Rule of Majority
    3. Responsible Government
    4. Legislative and Executive cooperation
    5. Leadership of PM/CM
    6. Collective responsibility of ministers
    7. Dissolution of Lower House

Notes: Indian Parliament is not a sovereign body like the British Parliament.
Indian State has an elected head (republic) while the British State has hereditary head (monarchy)

Parliamentary Sovereignty and Judicial Supremacy taken from UK and USA
A.    Parliament Sovereignty: British
1. Indian parliament can only amend constitution bound by certain rules, otherwise laws can be declared haywire by SC

B.    Judicial Supremacy: USA
1. Due process of Law followed in USA
1.     Law established by rule
2.     Moral check of law
2. But in India only Process Established by Law

Integrated and Independent Judiciary
A.    India: Single Court System
1.SC at the top, then HC, then district courts
2.Security of tenure,
3.Fixed service conditions,
4.All the Expenses of the Supreme Court charged on the Consolidated Fund of India,
5.Prohibition on discussion on the conduct of judges in the legislatures
6.Ban on practice after retirement
7.Power to punish for its contempt vested in the Supreme Court
8.Separation of the judiciary from the executive

B.    USA: Double Court System
1. Federal laws are enforced by the Federal judiciary
2. State laws are enforced by the State judiciary
