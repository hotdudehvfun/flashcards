FINANCIAL COMMITTEES
Public Accounts Committee 
  1. formed in 1921 provision by GOI Act 1919
  2. 22=15+7 members
  3. members elected for 1 year by STV
  4. a minister is not allowed on committee
  5. chairman selected by speaker from members
  6. after 1967, chairman is from opposition party
  7. examines the reports submitted by CAG
  8. CAG submits appropriation accounts, audit report on finance accounts and audit report on public undertakings. 
Detailed functions of PAC
  1. examine appropriation accounts laid before lok sabha
  2. scrutinise reports of CAG
  3. examine the accounts of state corporations
  4. examine the accounts of autonomous and semi-autonomous bodies, the audit of which is conducted by the CAG 
  5. examine the money spent on any service during a financial year in excess of the amount granted by the Lok Sabha for that purpose 

Limitations of PAC
(a) Not concerned with the questions of policy in broader sense
(b) conducts a post-mortem 
(c) cannot intervene in the matters of day-to-day administration
(d) recommendations are advisory only 
(e) not vested with the power of disallowance of expenditures
(f) Not an executive body and hence, cannot issue an order.


Estimates Committee
  1. since 1921
  2. first post independent 1950 recommended by John Mithai, finance minister
  3. till 1956 25, later 30 members all from Lok Sabha
  4. elected from lok sabha members by STV
  5. minister not allowed
  6. main function is examine budget and economic policies
Detail Functions of Committee
  1. To report what economies, improvements in organisation, efficiency and administrative reform consistent with the policy underlying the estimates, can be affected 
  2. To suggest alternative policies in order to bring about efficiency and economy in administration
  3. To examine whether the money is well laid out within the limits of the policy implied in the estimates
  4. To suggest the forth in which the estimates are to be presented to Parliament

The demands for grants may be finally voted despite the fact that the Committee has made no report

Limitations of Committee
(a) only alter budget grants voted 
(b) Cannot question the policy laid down by the Parliament
(c) advisory nature
(d) It examines every year only certain selected ministries and departments. Thus, by rotation, it would cover all of them over a number of years
(e) CAG does not assist Estimate committee, available to the Public Accounts Committee
(f) Nature of a post-mortem


Committee on Public Undertakings 
  1. since 1964, by Krishna Menon Committee
  2. 22 =15+7
  3. same as other financial committee,  no minister allowed
  4. chairman from lok sabha, not form rajya sabha
Functions
  1. examine accounts of public undertakings
  2. examine reports of CAG
  3. examine autonomy and efficiency of public undertakings

Limitations
  1. major govt. policy outside the purview of committee
  2. cannot interfere in day to day business
  3. matters for which already machinery established by special statute
  4. limited to upto 12 public undertakings in a year
  5. like all committees post-mortem work
  6. does not look into technical matters
  7. recommendations are advisory in nature


DEPARTMENTAL STANDING COMMITTEES 
  1. 24 committees, 16 in lok sabha, 8 works in rajya sabha
  2. 31=21+10
  3. no minister allowed
Main Functions
  1. consider demand of grants before presenting to vote in lok sabha
  2. reports cannot suggest cut motions
  3. examine bills, annual reports, 
  4. consider national basic long-term policies
Limitations for main functions
  1. no day to day business
  2. should not take matters which are already under other committees
  3. advisory nature
Functions related to Consideration of Demands Grants
  1. after general discussion, houses shall be adjourned
  2. make reports in time
  3. demands for grants shall be consider in house in lights of prepared reports
  4. separate reports for each demand
Limitations while considering grantsc
  1. Consider general principles and clauses of bill
  2. consider only those bills which are to referred in house
  3. make reports in given time

Benefits of Having Standing Committees
  1. they are not party bias
  2. flexible
  3. this system helps parliament to have greater control over executive
  4. ensures efficiency in public expenditure
  5. platform where any member(not minister) can take part and understand functioning of govt. and contribute to it
  6. committees can invite expert opinions which may lack in existing members
  7. rajya sabha members can also play part in exercising control over financial matters



COMMITTEES TO INQUIRE 
  1. Petitions: 15 + 10 members
  2. Privileges: 15+ 10 members, semi-judicial
  3. Ethics: 2000 lok sabha, in 1997 rajya sabha


COMMITTEES TO SCRUTINIZE AND CONTROL 
  1. Government assurance: 15 LS,10 RS
  2. Subordinate Legislation: 15 LS, 15 RS
  3. Paper Laid on Table: 15 LS, 10 RS
  4. Welfare of SCs and STs: 30=20+10, consider reports of National commission for SCs and STs
  5. Empowerment of Women: 20+10, consider reports of National Commission for Women
  6. Joint Committee on Offices of Profit : 15=10+5, 



COMMITTEES RELATING TO THE DAY-TO-DAY BUSINESS OF THE HOUSE

1. Business Advisory Committee 
  1. time table of house
  2. total 15 members: speaker as chairman
  3. rajya sabha: 11 members vice president as chairman, also handles private member bills
2. Private Members' Bills
  1. handles time allocation and manages bills introduced by private members
  2. lok sabha : 15 members: deputy speaker as chairman
  3. rajya sabha does not have this committee, task being done by business advisory committee
3. Rules Committee
  1. manages rules of houses
  2. lok sabha 15 members:speaker as chairman
  3. rajya sabha: 16 members : vp as chairman
4. Committee on Absence of members
  1. considers members who were absent for more than 60 days without permission
  2. only in lok sabha: 15 members
