Chapter 14 Center-State Relations
Legislative relations
1.  Parliament alone can make ‘extra-territorial legislation’
2. President can make regulations for the peace, progress and good government of the four Union Territories
the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, Lakshadweep, Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu
3. Governor can direct that certain laws of parliament cannot be applied to certain areas
4. Governor of Assam can also direct the same
5. President can make laws regarding tribal areas of Meghalaya, Tripura, Mizoram
6. Centre and state have separate lists but during emergency center takes over
42nd act transferred 5 subjects from state to concurrent list
(a) education
(b) forests
(c) weights and measures
(d) protection of wild animals and birds
(e) administration of justice; constitution and organisation of all courts except the Supreme Court and the high courts 
7. Residuary power with centre, also to levy residuary taxes
8. US federal powers in constitution, residuary with states
9. GOI Act 1935 provided three fold division

Exceptions when Centre makes laws on State List
1. Resolution passed by Rajya Sabha
passed by 2/3rd present and voting
for One year, can be passed again remains in force 6 months after resolution ceased to exists

2. National Emergency
State govt. is not suspended and can also make laws
remains in force 6 month after end of proclamation

3.  When State itself passes a resolution
state surrenders the power and only centre can make law on that subject
Laws under this scheme
Competition Act, 1955; 
Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972; 
Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act,1974; 
Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976
Transplantation of Human Organs Act, 1994

4. For implementing the international treaties, agreements or conventions
5. President's Rule
State govt. is suspended and governor rule is imposed
Law continues to be operative even after the president’s rule


Other provisions in which centre can control legislation of state
1. Governor reserved a bill for president, then president enjoys absolute veto, can return any number of times, withhold or take no action
2. Some subjects even on state lists requires president sanction
3. Financial emergency, president can reserved money bill


Sarkaria Commission on Centre–State Relations (1983–87)



ADMINISTRATIVE RELATIONS 
Executive power of state
(a) consistent with centre laws
(b) as not to impede or prejudice the exercise of executive power of the Centre in the state
Otherwise Art 365 can be used
Centre direction for state for following in which Art 365 can be used
1. Communication
2. Railways
3. Taught in mother-tongue
4. Welfare of Schedule Tribes

Mutual Delegation Function
Executive powers can be delegated from center to state and state to center
1. President consent with state, can delegate executive function of centre
2. Governor consent of centre, can delegate executive function of state
In above cases consent of party receiving the power has to consent
Special provision
Parliament without consent of state, can give state executive powers or duties. Reversal is not possible

Cooperation between Centre and States
1. Adjudication by parliament in case of inter-state disputes relating to rivers
2. President can form Inter-State council

Article 312 of the Constitution authorizes the Parliament to create new All-India Services on the basis of a Rajya Sabha resolution to that effect. 
IFS added in 1966
Chairman and members of the JSPSC are appointed by the president
State election commissioner, though appointed by the governor of the state, can be removed only by the President


FINANCIAL RELATIONS 
Allocation of Taxing Powers
1. Centre : 15 Subjects in Union List
2. State: 20 Subjects in state list
3. Both: 3 subjects in concurrent list
4. Residuary power with Centre: Gift tax, wealth, expenditure tax

Income-tax is levied and collected by the Centre but its proceeds are distributed between the Centre and the states. 
Service tax is levied by the centre but collected and appropriated by both the centre and the states
Restrictions on State
1.  impose taxes on professions, trades, callings and employments but 2500 per annum per person
2. tax on sale and purchase of goods, other than newspaper
a) not outside of state
b) not on import or export
c) not on inter-state trade
d) sale and purchase of good reserved by parliament to be of national importance
3. state can impose tax on electricity
but not on consume by centre, railway,  
4. state can impose tax on water or electricity sold by authority established by centre for inter-state river. but such law should be reserved for president

 80th Amendment effect to the recommendations of the 10th Finance Commission
29% of total income should go to the states
Alternative Scheme of Devolution effect retrospectively from April 1, 1996

Position of Taxes
A.  Levied by the Centre but Collected and Appropriated by the States (Article 268)
1. Stamp duties, policies of insurance
2. Excise duties on medicinal and toilet preparations containing alcohol and narcotics
B. Levied and Collected by the Centre but Assigned to the States (Article 269): 
1. Taxes on the sale or purchase of goods (other than newspapers) in the course of inter-state trade or commerce
2. Taxes on the consignment of goods in the course of inter-state trade or commerce
C. Levied and Collected by the Centre but Distributed between the Centre and the States (Article 270): 
1. Duties and taxes referred to in Articles 268, 268-A and 269 (mentioned above)
2. Surcharge on taxes and duties referred to in Article 271 (mentioned below)
3. Any cess levied for specific purposes
The manner of distribution of the net proceeds of these taxes and duties is prescribed by the President on the recommendation of the Finance Commission

D. Service Tax Levied by the Centre but Collected and Appropriated by the Centre and the States (Article 268-A): 
E. Surcharge on Certain Taxes and Duties for Purposes of the Centre (Article 271)
1. goes to centre only
F. Taxes Levied and Collected and Retained by the States 
1. only for state
(i) land revenue; 
(ii) taxes on agricultural income, succession and estate duties in respect of agricultural land;
(iii) taxes on lands and buildings, on mineral rights, on animals and boats, on road vehicles, on luxuries, on entertainments, and on gambling;
(iv) excise duties on alcoholic liquors for human consumption and narcotics; 
(v) taxes on the entry of goods into a local area, on advertisements (except newspapers), on consumption or sale of electricity, and on goods and passengers carried by road or on inland waterways; 
(vi) taxes on professions, trades, callings and employments not exceeding Rs. 2,500 per annum
(vii) capitation taxes; 
(viii) tolls; 
(ix) stamp duty on documents (except those specified in the Union List);
(x) sales tax (other than newspaper)
(xi) fees on the matters enumerated in the State List (except court fees). 


Grants-in-Aid to the States 
1. Article 275: Statutory Grants 
2. Article 282: Discretionary Grants 
3. Other Grants :  grants in lieu of export duties on jute and jute products to the States of Assam, Bihar, Orissa and west Bengal, charged on CFI

Finance Commission: Article 280
1. Distribution of the net proceeds of taxes to be shared between the Centre and the states, and the allocation between the states
2. The principles which should govern the grants-in-aid to the states by the Centre (i.e., out of the Consolidated Fund of India)
3. Augment the Consolidated fund of a state to supplement the resources of the panchayats and the municipalities in the state on the basis of the recommendations made by the State Finance Commission
4. Any other matter referred to it by the President in the interests of sound finance.

Protection of the States’ Interest 
Constitution requires president assent before presenting bill in parliament on following matters
1. imposes or varies tax which affects states
2. meaning of the expression ‘agricultural income’ as defined for the purposes of the enactments relating to Indian income tax
3. affects the principles on which money are or may be distributable to states
4. Imposes any surcharge on any specified tax or duty for the purpose of the Centre. 


Borrowing by the Centre and the States
1. centre can take loan or can give guarantees on CFI within india or abroad. limit fixed by parliament
2. state can borrow only within india on fund of state limit fixed by state legislature
3. centre can raise a loan charged on CFI
4. state cannot raise without consent of centre



Intergovernmental Tax Immunities
1. centre govt. property is exempt from state taxes
2. property and income of a state is exempted from Central taxation

Anandpur Sahib Resolution 1973
1. Akali Dal adopted a resolution containing both political and religious demands in a meeting held at Anandpur Sahib in Punjab
2. Centre powers restricted only to defence, foreign affairs, communications, and currency
3. rest with states and Constitution should be made federal in the real sense

West Bengal Memorandum 1977
1. replace union word with federal
2. Centre should be confined to defence, foreign affairs, currency, communications and economic co-ordination
3. residuary should be vested in the states
4. 356 and 357 (President’s Rule) and 360 (financial emergency) should be repealed
5. State’s consent should be made obligatory for formation of new states or reorganisation of existing states

Central government did not accept the demands made in the memorandum
