Chapter 1 Historical background

1934: M.N ROY suggested, need of constitution
1946: Constituent Assembly formed
1949: finally adopted Constitution 26th November

THE COMPANY RULE 1773-1853
  1. REGULATING ACT 1773
    1. Governor of Bengal upgraded to Gov.Gen: Hastings(1772-1785)
    2. Created Executive Council four members to assist him
    3. Bombay and Madras subordinate to Bengal
    4. Supreme Court at Calcutta (1774), 1 chief justice + 3 other judges
    5. Due to rising corruption, private trade, bribes, accepting presents banned
    6. COD to submit report on its Revenue, Civil, and Military Affairs to England
  2. Pitts ACT 1784
    1. Gov.-Gen: Hastings(1772-1785)
    2. Double Government system:Commercial and Political functions separate( will be ended by GOI Act 1857) 
    3. COD: Commercial Affairs
    4. BOC: Real Control, Political Affairs, Military, Revenue.
    5. First time:  Company’s territories in India were for the first time called the ‘British possessions in India’
  3. Charter ACT 1813
    1. Minto(1807-1813)
    2. Trade Monopoly of EIC ended
    3. Except for tea and trade with China
    4. financial provision for promotion of education and science
  4. Charter ACT 1833
    1. Gov-Gen: Bentick(1828-1835)
    2. EIC purely administrative company
    3. Bombay and Madras deprived of legislative powers( will restored by INC Act 1861)
    4. Previous acts :Regulations
    5. Under this acts: Acts.
    6. Attempted to introduce a system of Open Competition for selection of civil servants, and stated that the Indians should not be debarred from holding any place, office and employment under the Company. However, this provision was negated after opposition from the Court of Directors
  5. Charter ACT 1853
    1. Gov.-Gen: Dalhousie(1848-1856)
    2. Formed Legislative Council: ILC
    3. Separate Legislative and Executive functions of council, council functioned as mini-parliament on make policies and laws
    4. 6 new members to to council
    5. Local representation in ILC
    6. 4/6 new members, from local (provincial) governments of Madras, Bombay, Bengal and Agra
    7. Civil service through competitive exam
    8. Macaulay Committee(1854) (the Committee on the Indian Civil Service) 1854
    9. Extended the Company’s rule But, it did not specify any particular period

THE CROWN RULE (1858–1947)

  1. Government of India ACT 1858
    1. Governor-General upgraded to Viceroy: Canning
    2. Double Government: BOC and COD abolished, started by Pitt’s ACT 1784
    3. Office Secretary of State for India created:Complete authority and control over Indian administration
    4. 15-member Council Advisory nature assists Secretary of State
  2. Indian Council ACT 1861
    1. 3 Indians, Non-official entry to Council
    2. Raja of Banaras, the Maharaja of Patiala and Sir Dinkar Rao
    3. PORTFOLIO system started by CANNING in 1859
    4. Finally Decentralisation started
    5. Legislative powers of Bombay and Madras were restored(taken by Charter ACT 1833)
    6. New legislative councils formed : Bengal,  NWFP, Punjab, in 1862, 1866 and 1897 respectively
    7. Ordinances power of viceroy without concurrence of the legislative council, during an emergency. The life of such an ordinance was six months
  3. Indian Council ACT 1892
    1. More (non-official) members in councils by way of nominations, but maintained the official majority
    2. Legislative councils got power of discussing the budget, addressing questions to the executive
    3. Limited and Indirect provision for the use of election ,word [election] was not used
    4. Process was described as nomination made on the recommendation of certain bodies
  4. Indian Council ACT 1909
    1. Morley-Minto Reforms (Morley: Secretary, Minto: Viceroy)
    2. Increased the size of councils:  in Centre from 16 to 60, provincial not uniform
    3. Official majority in the Central, Provincial Non-official majority
    4. Councils got more powers, budget, ask questions
    5. Satyendra Prasad Sinha: Law member
    6. Muslims: ‘Separate Electorate’
    7. Lord Minto: Father of Communal Electorate
  5. Government of India ACT 1919
    1. August 20, 1917, Government declared: Introduction of responsible government in India
    2. Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms (Montagu: Secretary, Chelmsford: Viceroy)
    3. Central and provincial allowed making laws List of subjects
    4. Provincial subjects into two parts:
    5. DYARCHY: Double Rule of list.
      1. Transferred : RESPONSIBLE to Council
      2. Reserved: WITHOUT RESPONSIBLE to Council
    6. Bicameralism and direct elections in the country
      1. Upper House (Council of State)
      2. Lower House (Legislative Assembly)
    7. Direct Election: 3/6 were to be INDIANS in Viceroy’s executive Council
    8. Extended Communal Representation
      1. Sikhs, Indian Christians, Anglo-Indians and Europeans
    9. Franchise to a limited number of people on the basis of property, tax or education
    10. High Commissioner for India in London created
    11. Public service commission, Central Public Service Commission set up in 1926
    12. Budgets Separation, Central and Provincial
    13. Appointment of a statutory commission to inquire into law: 1927 Simon Commission 2 years before its schedule
  6. Simon Commission 1927
    1. 7 members under Sir John Simon arrived 3 Feb 1928 Bombay
    2. Opposed by INC and Muslim League
    3. 1927 Congress Session: Madras, President: M.A. Ansari
    4. Report
      1. Abolition of DYARCHY
      2. Extension of Responsible government in the provinces
      3. Proposed Federation  of British India and princely states
      4. Continuation of Communal Electorate
  7. 1928 Motilal Nehru : Nehru Report
    1. Dominion Status  and Self-government
    2. 1928 Congress Session Calcutta: Motilal Nehru
    3. Further
      1. Three round table
      2. ‘White Paper on Constitutional Reforms’ was prepared and submitted for the consideration of the Joint Select Committee of the British Parliament
  8. Government of India Act of 1935
    1. Second milestone towards a completely responsible government in India
    2. 321 Sections and 10 Schedules
    3. All-India Federation establishment
    4. Three lists
      1. Federal List (for Centre, with 59 items)
      2. Provincial List (for provinces, with 54 items)
      3. Concurrent List (for both, with 36 items)
    5. Residuary powers: Viceroy
    6. Abolished Dyarchy at provinces but continued at Centre
    7. Provincial Autonomy
    8. Introduced responsible governments: Governor to Act with the advice of ministers, came into effect in 1937 and was discontinued in 1939
    9. Federal subjects: RESERVED & TRANSFERRED subjects. However, this provision of the Act did not come into operation at all
    10. Bicameralism in six out of eleven provinces, Bengal, Bombay, Madras, Bihar, Assam and the United Provinces were made bicameral consisting of a legislative council (upper house) and a legislative assembly (lower house).
    11. Communal representation extended: Depressed Classes (scheduled castes), women and labour (workers)
    12. Abolished the Council of India, established by the Government of India Act of 1858
    13. The secretary of state for India was provided with a team of advisors
    14. Extended Franchise. almost 10% population
    15. RBI control the currency and credit of the country
    16. Establishment of:
      1. Federal Public Service Commission
      2. Provincial Public Service Commission
      3. Joint Public Service Commission for two or more provinces.
    17. Establishment Federal Court, which was set up in 1937
  9. Indian Independence Act of 1947
    1. February 20, 1947, Atlee British rule to end by June 30, 1948, transfer of power to responsible Indian hands
    2. Muslim League demanded partition
    3. June 3, 1947, Constitution framed by the Constituent Assembly of India (formed in 1946) cannot apply to those parts of the country which were unwilling to accept it
    4. June 3, 1947, Mountbatten Plan came
    5. Accepted by Congress and Muslim League
    6. Ended the British rule
    7. India as an independent and sovereign
    8. Provided for the partition of India and creation India and Pakistan with the right to secede from the British Commonwealth
    9. Abolished the office of Viceroy
    10. Empowered Constituent Assemblies of the two to frame and adopt any constitution and to repeal any act of the British Parliament, including the Independence act itself
    11. Empowered the Constituent Assemblies of both to legislate till the new constitutions were drafted and enforced
    12. No Act of the British Parliament passed after August 15, 1947 was to extend to either of the new dominions unless it was extended thereto by a law of the legislature of the dominion.
    13. Abolished the Office of the Secretary of State( created by GOI Act 1858)
    14. States either to join the Dominion of India or Dominion of Pakistan or to remain independent
    15. Government of India Act of 1935, till the new Constitutions 
    16. Deprived the British Monarch of his right to veto bills or ask for reservation of certain bills for his approval. But, this right was reserved for the Governor-General
    17. Governor-General would have full power to assent to any bill in the name of His Majesty
    18. It designated the Governor-General of India and the provincial governors as constitutional (nominal) heads of the states
    19. They were made to act on the advice of the respective council of ministers in all matters
    20. Title of Emperor of India from the royal titles of the king of England dropped
    21. It discontinued the appointment to civil services and reservation of posts by the secretary of state for India. The members of the civil services appointed before August 15, 1947 would continue to enjoy all benefits that they were entitled to till that time.
