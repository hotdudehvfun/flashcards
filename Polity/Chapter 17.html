Article 52-78 Part V deals with executive of Union

President

Election of President by Election Commission
Voters: 
a) Only Elected members of Parliament
b) Only Elected members of State Legislative Assembly
c) Elected members of  Delhi and Puducherry Assembly
if assembly is dissolved, cannot take part in election

Method: Single Transferable Vote
Value of MLAs vote: 
=P/(E*1000)
P=population of state
E=total elected members

Value of MP: ratio of value of all MLAs of each state: total Elected Seats in Parliament
=Value/Seats

Quota: (Total valid votes/(1+1))+1

Doubts and Disputes: SC, final decision
if SC declares election void, acts done by President in officer, remains in force


Qualifications for Election as President 
Citizen of India, 35 years age
Qualified for Lok Sabha
No Office of profit
NOTE: President, Vice-President, Governor, Minister of the Union or any state does not come under office of Profit.
Hence qualified as a presidential candidate.
Candidate must have 50 propers and 50 seconders
15,000 rupees deposit, won't get back if failed to get 1/6th of votes
 

OATH
by CJI or senior most judge of SC

While in office, immune from any criminal proceedings, even in respect of his personal acts
NO arrested or imprisoned
but, after giving two months’ notice, civil proceedings can be instituted against him during his term of office in respect of his personal acts


Term of President’s Office 
5 years, can hold office until successor comes

POWERS AND FUNCTIONS OF THE PRESIDENT 

A. EXECUTIVE POWERS
1. rules manner in which orders and instruments made
2. rules for transaction of business
3. appoints, PM and council on PMs advice, and other constitutional posts
4. can seek information from PM
5. cann appoint commission to investigate conditions of ST, SC and OBCs
6. can appoint inter-state council
7. can directly administers UTs
8. can declare any area as schedule area for tribals

B. LEGISLATIVE POWERS
1. summon/prorogue parliament, can dissolve lok sabha, can summon joint sitting 
2. address parliament first session/first session of year
3. can send message regarding pending bill
4. can appoint member to preside as speaker/chairman of rajya sabha if seat falls vacant
5. 2 anglo-indians / 12 nominated to R.S by president
6. Decides on disqualification of members, consult by EC
7. some bills requires his consent, money bill, state alteration bill
8. bills again sent by parliament cannot be returned but bills of state again sent by governor can be returned
9. promulgate ordinances when parliament not in session. after parliament meets has to pass in 6 week
10. UPSC, CAG, FC submits their report to him and he lay down in front of parliament
11. can me rules regarding Andaman, lakshadweep, dadra, daman. and also puducherry when assembly is dissolved



C. FINANCIAL POWERS
1. Money bill required prior consent
2. lays budget
3. no demand of grant without his consent
4. advances out of contingency fund
5. constitutes finance commission


D. JUDICIAL POWERS
1. appoints CJI, SC judges and HC judges

3. Pardoning powers

ORDINANCE MAKING POWER OF PRESIDENT
Article 123: power of president to promulgate ordinances 
1. President can promulgate ordinances when parliament not in session only on advice of council headed by PM. Power is not discretionary
a) power to withdraw ordinances is also requires advice of council headed by PM.
2. Temporary laws
a) but when parliament meets, it has to be passed within 6 weeks of meeting, else law expires
b) so maximum time a ordinance can get is 6 months to meet and another 6 week to pass
c) parliament can also disapprove it by passing resolutions
3. Only when no or one house in session
4. Cooper Case 1970:Ordinance making power is up for judicial review 
38th 1975:  ordinance by president beyond judicial review
44th 1978: deleted above, yes for judicial review
5. On same subjects on which parliament can make laws, hence ordinances are bound by constitutional rules
6. An ordinance can
a) be retrospective, alter tax, repeal an act, 
but cannot amend constitution

D C Wadhwa case 1987
SC said re promulgation of ordinances without any change and without any attempt to get passed by assembly would amount to violation of constitution.

Pardoning Power Article 72, Article 161 Governor
Requirement: Under Union law, court martial, death
1. pardoning power is an executive power
2. president does not sit as a court of appeal
a) Pardon: complete absolve
b) Commutation: rigorous to lighter, death to life imprisonment
c) Remission: reduce periods
d) Respite: Lesser sentence
e) Reprieve: stay on execution

Article 161: Governor can pardon only on state laws. cannot pardon court martial, and death sentence. however can take other actions on death sentence.

SC laid down following principles
1. no right to oral hearing
2.. president can examine evidence afresh
3. power to be exercised  on advice of union.
4. not bound to giver reason.
5. can afford relief not only from a sentence that he regards as unduly harsh but also from an evident mistake
6.  no need for the Supreme Court to lay down specific guidelines 
7.  exercise of power by the President is not subject to judicial review except where the presidential decision is arbitrary,irrational, malafide or discriminatory
8.  Where the earlier petition for mercy has been rejected by the President, stay cannot be obtained by filing another petition

CONSTITUTIONAL POSITION OF THE PRESIDENT
1. President is only head of state, a nominal head, in whose name laws are executed but he is not the head of executive
2. Real power is exercised by council headed by pm
3.  Article 53: executive power veseted in president shall be exercised by him directly or through officers under him
4. Article 74: there shall be a council head by PM, and president shall exercise functions in accordance of council
5. Article 75: Council of ministers shall be collectively responsible

President has no constitutional discretion
Situational discretion
 President can act on his discretion (that is, without the advice of the ministers) under the following situations: 
(i) Appointment of Prime Minister when no party has a clear majority in the Lok Sabha or when the Prime Minister in office dies suddenly and there is no obvious successor. 
(ii) Dismissal of the council of ministers when it cannot prove the confidence of the Lok Sabha. 
(iii) Dissolution of the Lok Sabha if the council of ministers has lost its majority.
