3.    Rigid and Flexible Constitution

A.    Article 368

1.Special majority of the Parliament, i.e., A two-third majority present and voting plus a majority (that is, more than 50 per cent), of the total membership of each House.

2.A Special Majority of the Parliament and with the Ratification by Half of the Total States.

NOTE: A Simple Majority of the Parliament in the manner of ordinary legislative process.

Notably, these amendments Do Not Come under Article 368

7.    Integrated and Independent Judiciary

1.SC at the top, then HC, then district courts

2.Security of tenure,

3.Fixed service conditions,

4.All the Expenses of the Supreme Court charged on the Consolidated Fund of India,

5.Prohibition on discussion on the conduct of judges in the legislatures

6.Ban on practice after retirement,

7.Power to punish for its contempt vested in the Supreme Court,

8.Separation of the judiciary from the executive

B.    USA: Double Court System

1.Federal laws are enforced by the Federal judiciary

2.State laws are enforced by the State judiciary
