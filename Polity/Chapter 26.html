Chapter 26 Supreme Court Article 124 to 147

India single system of courts
Government of India Act of 1935, enforces both Central laws as well as the state laws
USA separate courts for state and federal : Double Court System,

SC inaugurated 28 Jan 1950, established by GOI Act 1935
SC replaced British Privy Council

Organisation
  1. Present: 31=1 CJI + 30 Judges
Judges of SC
  1. appointed by President after consulting Collegium of CJI and senior Judges
  2. consultation is binding on president
Controversy over Consultations

1. First Judges Case 1982
a) Opinion is not binding
2. Second Judges Case 1993
a) Opinion is binding but CJI should consult 2 senior judges of SC
3. Third Case 1998
a) court said now CJI should consult 4 judges and even if 2 of them says no then names should not be sent. 2 in case of HC judge appointment
4. Fourth Case 2015
a) parliament 99th Act 2014 brought National judicial appointments commission act and abolished collegium system
b) SC said 99th act is unconstitutional and invalid, hence collegium system is in place



Appointment of CJI
  1. 1950-1973 convention of selecting CJI as senior most judge
  2. but in 1973 A.N Ray was chosen over three other senior judges
  3. also 1977 M.U Beg was chosen over other
  4. 1993 SC in Second judge case said only senior most should be chosen


Qualifications of SC Judges
  1. citizen
  2. 10 year lawyer in HC
  3. or 5 year judge of SC
  4. distinct jurist in opinion of president
  5. no minimum age
Oath
  1. Administered by President

Tenure
  1. till 65 age
  2. resigns to president
  3. Removal: Same SC/HC judge(motion,committee,address)
    1. Removal motion signed by 100 if rises in lok sabha and 50 if charges are raised in Rajya Sabha.
    2. Speaker may or may not admit motion
    3. After that, 3 member committee investigates(CJI/SC judge, CJ of HC, 1 Jursit)
    4. then motion has to be passed by special majority in both houses
    5. Judge is removed.
  4. Grounds: misbehaviour or incapacity
  5. Judges Enquiry Act 1968 regulated procedure to remove
Acting CJI
  1. president can appoint SC judge as acting CJI when
  2. office is vacant
  3. temporarily absent
  4. CJI cannot perform duty
Ad Hoc Judges
  1. if lack of quorum in SC, CJI can appoint HC judge as an ad hoc judge in SC
  2. requires approval of CJ of that HC, and consent of President
  3. after that a high court judge can sit in SC 
Retired Judges
  1. CJI can request a retired judge of SC or HC to act as a judge of SC for temp period
  2. requires consent of person and president
Seat of SC
  1. constitution declares delhi as seat of SC
  2. but CJI can appoint other place as seat of SC with approval of president
  3. optional not compulsory to change seat

SC with approval of president can make rules
Article 143: bench of 5 judges can give non binding advice to president on matters referred to it.

Powers of SC
1. Original Jurisdiction. 
2. Writ Jurisdiction. 
3. Appellate Jurisdiction. 
4. Advisory Jurisdiction. 
5. A Court of Record. 
6. Power of Judicial Review. 
7. Other Powers

1. Original Jurisdiction. 
  1. original means to hear dispute in first instance not by appeal
  2. Covers:
  3. Centre vs State
  4. Centre+State vs States
  5. State vs States
  6. Rules:
  7. matter of legal right not political nature
  8. should not be of private citizen against Centre/State
  9. does not cover pre-constitution treaty
  10. does not cover inter-state water disputes
  11. does not cover matters of finance commission
  12. does not cover expenses and pension between centre and states
  13. does not cover matters of commercial nature
  14. does not cover recovery of damages by a state against centre


2. Writ Jurisdiction
  1. SC has original jurisdiction to issue writs in FR
  2. 5 writs: habeas corpus, mandmas, prohibition, quo-warrento, ceritorari
  3. SC has original but not exclusive, HC can also issue writs for FR and also for other purposes.

3. Appellate Jurisdiction
  1. SC can also hear disputes way of Appeal
  2. a) Constitutional Matters
  3. against the order HC, and HC certify that law requires interpretation of SC
  4. b) Civil Matters
  5. cases in which HC certify that case involved substantial question of law
  6. needs to be decided by SC
  7. c) Criminal Matters
  8. SC can hear appeals in criminal matters,against the order of HC if
  9. HC reversed that order of acquittal and sentence the person to death, 10 years
  10. HC taken from subordinate court, and sentence person to death, 10 years
  11. certify that fit for SC
  12. if there is death sentence,appeal can be done as a matter of right
  13. d) Appeal by Special Leave
  14. Discretionary and Exceptional power of SC, to hear appeals by leave
  15. it is a discretionary power, not a right  
  16. can be granted in final or interlocutory judgement
  17. can be related to constitutional, civil, criminal etc but no military court
  18. Scope of appeal by Special leave is wide and exceptional

4. Advisory Jurisdiction
  1. Article 143: president can take advice of SC
  2. on any question of law of public importance: SC may refuse
  3. dispute out of pre-Constitution, treaty etc: SC must give advice
5. A Court of Record
  1. records are admitted to be of evidentiary value and cannot be questioned when produced before any court
  2. power to punish for contempt of court, either with simple imprisonment for a term up to six months or with fine up to 2,000 or with both
  3. contempt may be civil or criminal
  4. civil means breaking of civil laws, like disobedience
  5. criminal means : criminal act like interfere in court proceedings, scandalizing 
  6. however, criticize of facts in publication is not an offense
6. Power of Judicial Review
  1. Article 32,226

7. Other Powers
  1. decides disputes regarding president and vp elections
  2. can enquire into conduct of UPSC chairman and can advice president for removal, advice is binding
  3. can review its previous judgement
  4. can withdraw cases from HC and dispose them itself
  5. final interpreter of constitution


Indian vs American Supreme Court
1. original jurisdiction over federal case | original covers federal plus naval, maritime
2. appellate covers constitutional, civil, criminal, special leave | confined only to constitutional cases
3. power to grant special leave | no such powers in US SC
4. Article 143: advisory jurisdiction | no such provision in US SC
5. judicial review in India is smaller than US SC. In India, due process is not followed, only laws established by laws, no morality check. But in USA there is due process of law
6. powers can be increase by parliament but cannot curtail | powers limited to that of constitution
7. SC power over whole country | no such power, because there is double system
