Basic Structure of Constitution Drama involving between SC and Parliament 


Case 1: Gopalan case (1950)
        1. the Supreme Court has taken a narrow interpretation of the Article 21


Case 2. Champakam Dorairajan case (1951)
       SC said FR>DPSP
       Fundamental Rights can be amended

Case 3: Shankari Prasad case (1951)
        1. Constitutional validity of the First Amendment Act (1951), which curtailed the right to property, was challenged
        2. SC said parliament can amend FR under Article 368. 
        3. Word 'law' in Article 13 includes only ordinary laws and not the constitutional amendment acts (constituent laws)
        4. Therefore, the Parliament can abridge or take away any of the Fundamental Rights by enacting a constitutional amendment act and such a law will not be void under Article 13.


Case 4: Golak Nath case (1967)
1. 17th Amendment Act (1964): state acts in the Ninth Schedule, was challenged
2. SC, reversed its decision and  said FR cannot be amended.
3. Constitutional amendment act is also a law within the meaning of Article 13 and hence, would be void for violating any of the Fundamental Rights.

Parliament reaction
1. 24th Amendment 1971
Amended Articles 13 and 368
Also declared parliament can take away FR under Article 368 and such an act will not be a law under the meaning of Article 13

2. 25th Amendment Act
Inserted Article 31C
        1. to give effect to Article 39 (b) and (c),  Article 14, Article 19, Article 31 can be curtailed
        2. Not up for judicial review

Case 5: Kesavananda Bharati case (1973)
        1. SC changed its stance on taking away of FR
        2. Introduced "Basic Structure", upheld validity of 24th Amendment and said parliament can curtail FR as long as it does not destroy "Basic Structure"
        3. Invalid 2nd provision( DPSP>FR to give effect to Art 39b and c which protected it from judicial review ) of 25th Amendment act. judicial review is a basic structure. 



Case 6: Indira Nehru Gandhi case (1975)
1. SC invalidated a provision of the 39th Amendment Act (1975) which kept the election disputes involving the Prime Minister and the Speaker of Lok Sabha outside the jurisdiction of all courts.
2. The court said that this provision was beyond the amending power of Parliament as it affected the basic structure of the constitution.

Parliament Reaction: 42nd Amendment Act (1976). 
1. Amended Article 368 and declared that there is no limitation on the constituent power of Parliament and no amendment can be questioned in any court on any ground including that of the contravention of any of the Fundamental Rights

        2. Right to life is not just animal existence of human being. other factors also considered.
