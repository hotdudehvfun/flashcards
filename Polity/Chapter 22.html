TWO HOUSES

Rajya Sabha
  1. Max: 250=238 (States,UTs) + 12(nominated)
  2. Present: 245=Elected 233:(229(States)+4(delhi:3 Puducherry:1 ))+ 12 nominated
  3. Seats are in proportion to population, but in USA each states get 2 seats
  4. 1/3rd retire every 2 years
  5. first constituted in 1952, permanent body
  6. Term of office is not fixed, to be decided by parliament
  7. Representation of People Act 1951
  8. fixed term for 6 years
  9. empowered president to curtail term of members
  10. in first batch, retirement was decided by lottery
  11. order of retirement by president
  12. minimum age 30, oath under person authorized by EC
  13. must be registered as an elector
Lok Sabha
  1. Max: 552: 530 states, 20 UTs, 2 nominated
  2. Present: 545: 530 states, 13 UTs, 2 nominated
  3. after 5 years, automatically dissolves
  4. can be extended beyond 5 years for 6 months at a time under article 352, resolution passed by both house special majority in 1 month
  5. min age 25, oath under person authorized by EC
  6. must be registered as elector

Voting age was reduced from 21 to 18 years by the 61st Constitutional Amendment Act, 1988

Territorial Constituencies
  1. Each state gets seats in Lok sabha, ratio of number to population is same for all,
  2. Each state is divided into territorial constituencies in such a manner that the ratio between the population of each constituency and the number of seats allotted to it is the same throughout the state 
  3. Constitution ensures that there is uniformity of representation in two respects: (a) between the different states, and (b) between the different constituencies in the same state


Readjustment after each Census
  1. Parliament has enacted the Delimitation Commission Acts in 1952, 1962, 1972 and 2002 
  2. 42nd 1976 froze allocation till 2000 at 1971 level
  3. 84th 2001 readjustment on 1991 census
Single transferable vote system is used for rajya sabha, president, vp, legislative council

Vacating of Seats
Double membership:
  1. a person elected to both house, intimate within 10 days else Rajya Sabha seats get vacant
  2. already member of one house, gets seat in other house, seat of first house gets vacant
  3. person gets two seats in one house, he must choose one else both gets vacant.
Member can give resign to speak or chairman
Absence without permission for 60 days

If a disqualified person gets elected, no provision in constitution to declare election void.
According to RPA 1951, matter is dealt by High Court

A person is liable to a penalty of Rs 500 for each day he sits or votes as a member in a House in the following conditions:
  1. Before taking and subscribing to the prescribed oath or affirmation
  2. When he knows that he is not qualified or that he is disqualified for its membership
  3. When he knows that he is prohibited from sitting or voting in the House by virtue of any parliamentary law

Speaker of Lok Sabha
Election
  1. among members of lok sabha
  2. date fixed by president
Tenure
  1. normally 5 years but holds office until newly elected lok sabha meets
Vacant Office
  1. Speaker is not a member of lok sabha
  2. Resigns to deputy speaker
  3. Removal by resolution passed by lok sabha by effective majority( majority of effective strength), supported by 50 members.
  4. In removal can sit and can vote in first instance
Powers
  1. Source of power: Constitution, rules of business, residuary powers
  2. Maintains order and decorum
  3. final interpreter of provisions
  4. Adjourns house or Suspends in absence of quorum
  5. Cast a tie vote to resolve deadlock
  6. Presides over joint sitting
  7. Can allow for a secret meeting
  8. Endorses a bill as a money bill
  9. decides disqualifications matters
  10. Appoints chairman of parliamentary committees, himself chairman of business advisory committee 
  11. salaries charged on CFI
  12.  work and conduct cannot be discussed and criticised in the Lok Sabha except on a substantive motion
  13. placed at 7th rank along with CJI, higher rank than cabinet

Election

Motions
To discuss any matter

Categories of motions
1. Substantive Motion
very important, like impeachment of president, removal of CEC.
2. Substitute Motion:
in place of original
3. Subsidiary Motion
reference to other motion
1. ancillary(supporting): regular way of proceeding
2. superseding: seeks to supersede another issue
3. amendment: modify original motion

1. CLOSURE MOTION: cut short (close discussion)

  2. closure by compartments: lengthy , group into parts
  3. kangaroo closure: only important clauses
  4. guillotine closure: due to less time everything put to vote
2. PRIVILEGE MOTION

3. CALLING ATTENTION MOTION

  3. time 2.5 hours
  4. should raise a definite matter

Main Types of Bill
  1. Private:
1. private member, reflects policies of opposition
2. less chance to approve
3. required 1 month notice
4. drafting by concerned member
  1. Public
1. by minister
2. rejection may lead to resignation
3. requires 7 day notice

Types of Bills
  1. Ordinary bills: other than financial subjects
  2. Money Bill: tax, public expenditure, CFI
  3. Financial bills: FB-I and FB-II
  4. Amendment Bills

Process for ORDINARY BILLS
The Five Stages(1,2,3,other,president)
  1. FIRST READING(intro and publication in gazette)
1. in either house
2. by any member, member has to ask for leave of house
3. after grant of leave, title and subject is read
4. bill is published in Gazette of India 

   2. SECOND READING(three stages detailed scrutiny)
1. General Discussion( give to committee)
1. may take bill into consideration 
2. or refer to select(members of house where bill is born) committee
3. or refer to joint(both houses) committee
4. public opinion
    2. Committee Stage:scrutiny and discussion
    3. Consideration Stage:clause by clause discussion and voting

   3. Third Reading : accept or reject voting
   4. Bill in Second House
1. again first, second, third reading
2. may accept/reject, amend, pending
   5. Assent of President
1. withhold
2. assent
3. return for correction
4. no action

Process for Money Bills(Article 110)
Rules when a bill is money bill
  1. impose, abolish, alter TAX
  2. Borrowing money by centre
  3. appropriation/payment/expenditure form consolidated or contingency fund
  4. receipt on public account
When a bill deals with ONLY following is not a MONEY Bill
  1. fines, penalties
  2. payment of fees, licenses, services
  3. abolish, alter, regulate tax by local body for local purpose
Final decision by Speaker, cannot be challenged in any court
Special Rules for money bill
  1. introduction only in Lok Sabha, only by minister and that too by prior permission of president
  2. rajya sabha cannot reject or amend
  3. when sent to rajya sabha, speaker endorse it as money bill
  4. it can only suggest changes, within 14 days
  5. when goes to president, he can withhold or assent but cannot return

Financial Bills
Types
  1. money bills(article 110)
  2. Financial bill I(article 117)
  3. Financial bills II (article 117)
Financial Bills I
  1. contains matter of article 110 and also general legislation
  2. example: bill deals with burrowing but not exclusively and also deals with other general matters
  3. only difference : rajya sabha can reject or amend FB-I
  4. all rules of ordinary bill applies here
  5. similar to money bill: only lok sabha prior permission of president
  6. joint sitting is possible, as rajya sabha can reject it

Financial Bills II
  1. contains expenditure from CFI but does not include any matter of article 110
  2. all ordinary bill rules applies to it
  3. The only special feature of this bill is that it cannot be passed by either House of Parliament unless the President has recommended to that House the consideration of the bill
  4. Can be introduced without president prior permission



JOINT SITTING OF TWO HOUSES
By president, presided by speaker
Situations
  1. bill rejected by other house
  2. conflict over accepting changes
  3. more than 6 months pending
General points for joint sitting
  1. Only for ordinary bills, not for money, amendments because has to be passed by house separately
  2. If lok sabha is dissolved no joint sitting, but if dissolved after president is notified, joint sitting will take place and no house can further take up bill
  3. order of precedence for presiding join sitting is speaker,deputy speaker, deputy chairman of rajya sabha
  4. vice president/chairman of rajya sabha cannot preside as he is not a member of parliament
  5. Quorum required id 10% of total members of both
  6. Rules to be followed : Lok Sabha
  7. only those amendments will be accepted at join sitting, which caused the conflict and those which became necessary due to delay
  8. So Far three times
  9. dowry prohibition 1960
  10. banking service 1977
  11. prevention of terrorism, 2002

MULTI FUNCTIONAL ROLE OF PARLIAMENT 

1. Legislative Powers and Functions 
2. Executive Powers and Functions 
3. Financial Powers and Functions 
4. Constituent Powers and Functions 
5. Judicial Powers and Functions 
6. Electoral Powers and Functions 
7. Other powers and functions. 

1. Legislative Powers: convert bills into acts
  1. make laws through bills(a 5 stage process): 1(),2,3,other house, president assent
  2. parliament has exclusive power to make laws on Union list(100)
  3. Residuary power on Concurrent list(52)
  4. Overriding power on State list(47)
  5. Abnormal conditions when parliament can make laws on state
  6. a) president rule in state
  7. b) national emergency
  8. c) rajya sabha passes a resolution
  9. d) necessary to give effect to international agreements
  10. Delegation legislation: parliament passes bills to make acts, executive can form detailed laws within the boundary of Acts

2. Executive Powers: make sure laws are executed by making laws on basis of Acts passed by parliament
  1. council is collectively responsible 
  2. parliament keeps checks on executive
  3. lok sabha can remove government through
  4. not passing motion of thanks
  5. rejecting money bill
  6. no confidence motion: required 50 supporters
  7. passing cut motions, means no grants, means govt. cannot take money

3. Financial Powers
  1. parliament passes budget to grant money to executive from CFI
  2. budget passes through 6 stages
  3. a) present
  4. b) discussion
  5. c) scrutiny
  6. d) vote on grants: cut motions can be used by parliament to keep on check demands, only in lok sabha
  7. e) appropriation bill
  8. f) financial bill
  9. above stages proves that every penny spent is appropriated to avoid abuse of funds
  10. After budget is passed
  11. post-mortem can be done by parliament three committees:public account committee, estimate, public undertakings 
  12. also note funds are granted for 1 financial year only. unspent funds will go back to CFI. this is Rule of Lapse
  13. Effect of rule of lapse is march rush
4. Constituent Powers
  1. only parliament can amend constitution by three ways
  2. simple
  3. special (article 368)
  4. special+ratification by 50% states (article 368)
  5. however there checks on amending power of parliament
  6. judicial review
  7. article 32,226,13
  8. SC also mentioned basic features cannot be taken in Kesavananda case 1973
  9. also in Minerva Mills Case 1980
5. Judicial Powers 
  1. Impeachment of president
  2. 1/4th members sign charges, 2/3rd majority passed separately in each house
  3. removal of vice president:  resolution of the Rajya Sabha passed by an effective majority (i.e.,majority of all the then member of the house ) and agreed by the Lok Sabha with simple majority
  4. recommends removal of sc judges, CJI, high court judges, CEC, CAG
  5. punish members or outsiders for breach of privileges

6. Electoral Powers 
  1. elects president, vice president
  2. lok sabha speaker, deputy speaker
  3. chairman of rajya sabha, deputy chairman
  4. can also make laws regarding elections of above posts and also MPs


7. Other Powers 
  1. approval of all three types of emergencies proclaimed by president
  2. can approve or abolish ordinances passed by president
  3. can create or abolish legislative councils
  4. can change constituencies which will affects seats in parliament
  5. alter state boundaries
  6. can delegate powers to states after consent of states, and also can take powers of states in own hands

POSITION OF RAJYA SABHA
Equal Powers
  1. ordinary bills
  2. constitutional amendments
  3. election and removal of president, vice president: resolution by rajya sabha special and approved by simple majority by lok sabha
  4. recommendation for constitutional posts
  5. ordinances by president
  6. proclamation of emergencies
  7. pm can belong to any house
Unequal Status with Lok Sabha
  1. rajya sabha cannot take decision in all matters related to money
  2. money bill, vote on grants
  3. Can amend FB-I but it has to originate in lok sabha
  4. Can amend FB-II 
  5. chairman has nothing to do with if a bill is money bill or not
  6. chairman of rajya sabha is not a part of parliament, cannot preside over joint sitting
  7. Discontinue of National Emergency can only pass in Lok Sabha
  8. rajya sabha cannot move no confidence motion, or any cut motions



Special Powers of Rajya Sabha
  1. rajya sabha can pass resolution to make laws on state subjects
  2. it can authorize to make All-India Services common to both centre and states
