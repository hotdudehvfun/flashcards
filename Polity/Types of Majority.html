Absolute, Simple, Effective and Special(Article 249, 368)
Absolute majority
50% plus 1, not 2/3rd
Seats: 545, 50% of 545 plus 1, ie. 273.

Effective Majority: All the then members
50% plus 1 of effective strength of the house
Effective strength=total seats - seats not filled 
Total strength of 245: 
Not filled: 45
Effective strength 200
Effective majority is 50% of 200 plus 1, ie 101
Removal of Vice-president in RS( cause it is chairman of Rajya Sabha) – Article 67(b).
Removal of Speaker and Deputy Speaker of Lok Sabha and State Legislative Assembly.

Simple Majority
2/3rd of present and voting
Functional majority
Ordinary bills
Non-Confidence Motion/Adjournment Motion/Censure Motion/Confidence Motion.
Removal of Vice President in Lok Sabha is the simple majority – Article 67(b).
Financial emergency.
State emergency (President’s rule).
Election of Speaker/Deputy Speaker of Lok Sabha and State legislatures
Constitution Amendment Bill under Article 368 which needs to be ratified by states, require only simple majority at State Legislatures.

Special Majority
All types of majorities other than the absolute, effective or simple majority is known as the special majority.
4 Types
Type 1 – Special Majority as Per Article 249.
Type 2 – Special Majority as per Article 368.
Type 3 – Special Majority as per Article 368 + 50 percent state ratification by a simple majority.
Type 4 – Special Majority as per Article 61.

Special Majority as Per Article 249
2/3rd members present and voting
Article 249:Rajya Sabha resolution to empower the parliament to make laws in the state list. (valid up to 1 year, but can be extended any number of times).

Special Majority as Per Article 368
2/3rd members present and voting + 50% of the total strength of the house
Case:
Constitutional amendment bills.
Removal of judges of SC/HC.(motion,committee,address)
Removal of CEC/CAG.
National emergency requires special majority as per Article 368 in both houses.
Resolution by the state assembly for the creation/abolition of Legislative Council (Article 169)

Special Majority as Per Article 368 plus State ratification
Constitutional amendment bill try to change the federal structure
Special majority at centre level and simple majority
A good example would be the bill which introduced the National Judicial Appointments Commission (NJAC). It required the support of at least 15 state legislatures out of the 29 states.
Cases:
Constitutional amendment bill which affects federalism like the position of High Court Judges
Special Majority as Per Article 61
Special majority as per article 61 requires a majority of 2/3rd members of the total strength of the house
In Lok Sabha, the special majority as per article 61 is 364(not 51% but 66% ) while in Rajya Sabha, the special majority as per article 61 is 164.
Cases: For the impeachment of the Indian President(1/4th members sign charges,2/3rd,2/3rd pass)
