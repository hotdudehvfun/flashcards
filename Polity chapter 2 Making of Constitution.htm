Making of the Constitution

1934 Idea of constitution put forward by M.N Roy

1935 INC for the first time, officially demanded a Constituent Assembly

1938, Jawaharlal Nehru, on behalf the INC declared that ‘the Constitution of free India must be framed, without outside interference, by a Constituent Assembly elected on the basis of adult franchise’

1940 August Offer’ accepted the demand of constituent assembly

1942 Cripps Mission

o   Cabinet came to India with a draft proposal of the British Government on the framing of an independent Constitution to be adopted after the World War II

o   Rejected by both ML and INC

1946 Cabinet Mission Plan

o   Formed Constituent Assembly in Nov. 1936

o   Seats allocation: Muslims, Sikhs, General

o   Indirect election: single transferable vote

o   Partially elected partially nominated

o   1 seat per million

o   princely states were not filled as they decided to stay away from the Constituent Assembly

WORKING OF THE CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY

·        Dr Sachchidanand Sinha temporary President

·        Later Dr Rajendar Prasad

o   H.C. Mukherjee and V.T. Krishnamachari V-Ps

·        First meeting: December 9, 1946

·        Muslim League boycotted the meeting and insisted on a separate state of Pakistan

·        Objective Resolution

o   Moved by Nehru : Dec 1946

o   India as Independent Sovereign Republic

o   India shall be union of states

o   power and authority of the Sovereign Independent India derived from the people

o   Unanimously adopted by the Assembly on January 22, 1947

o   Modified version become Preamble

·        Sanctioned the India’s membership of the Commonwealth in May 1949

·        National flag: July 22, 1947.

·        National anthem: January 24, 1950.

·        National song: January 24, 1950

 

Changes by Indian Independence Act 1947

·        Assembly was fully Sovereign

·        Dual functions by Assembly

o   Making of Constitutions:  Dr. Rajendra Prasad

o   Legislative Functions: G V Mavlankar

The Constitution as adopted on November 26, 1949, contained a Preamble, 395 Articles and 8 Schedules

The Preamble was enacted after the entire Constitution was already enacted.

1930 that Purna Swaraj day was celebrated, following the resolution of the Lahore Session (December 1929) of the INC.
