Chapter 6 Administrative organisation and policy

After 1784 British Govt. controlled activities of EIC
Under HAstings, Cornwallis administration went under heavy changes to suit needs of govt. in england

Pillars of British Govt. 
1. Civil Services
  1. Reforms by Cornwallis(1786)
  2. EIC servants were low paids, hence they were corrupt
  3. Cornwallis reformed civil services to remove corruption
  4. he raised salaries
  5. But 1793: exclusions of Indians from higher posts
  6. 1800, Wellesley established College of Fort William: to train young civil servants
  7. Directors of Company disapproved this college
  8. 1806:replace it by their own East Indian College at Haileybury in England 
  9. Till 1853, appointments to Civil Service were made by Directors of EIC
  10. But Charter Act 1853: civil service through competitive exam

2. Army
  1. 1856: highest post to indian was that of subedar
3. Police
  1. Police Reform: Cornwallis
  2. Zamindars were taken off their police functions
  3. Introduced system of Thanas headed by Daroga
  4. Post of District Superintendent of Police was created
  5. Suppression of thugs and robbers

Judicial Organisation
  1. Started by HAstings, but final shape by Cornwallis in 1793
  2. Civil and Criminal Courts
  3. Diwani Adalat: Civil Court presided by District Judge
  4. Nizamat Adalat: Criminal
  5. Collector and Civil Judge was separated 
  6. Structure of Courts(Top to Bottom)
  7. Sadar Diwani Adalat>
  8. Four Provincial Courts>
  9. District Courts>
  10. Registrar's Courts headed by Europeans>
  11. Subordinate Courts headed by Indians: Munsifs and Amins
  12. Cornwallis divided bengal into four divisions
  13. Each division had Court of Circuit headed by civil servants
  14. Below these Indian Magistrates for petty cases
  15. they used modified Muslim Criminal Law in less harsh form
  16. Barbaric punishments were stopped
  17. 1831: Bentick: abolished provincial courts of appeal and circuit
  18. Bentinck raised positions of indian magistrates to position of Deputy Magistrates, Subordinate Judges, Sardar Amins
  19. 1865: HC established at Calcutta, Madras, Bombay to replace Sardar Courts of Diwani and Nizamat
  20. British codified existing laws
  21. Charter Act 1833: conferred law making power on Gov.Gen-in-council
  22. An era was started in which indians were to live under man made laws
  23. 1833: Law commission headed by Lord Macaulay to codify Indian Laws


The Rule of LAW
  1. Modern concept rule of law
  2. now official could be brought before court for breach of laws

Equality before law?
  1. Europeans had different courts and by European judge only


Social and Cultural Policies
  1. Till 1813 British did not interfere in religious and social lifes
  2. 1829 Sati Abolition Act
  3. 1856 Widow remarriage act after Ishwar C.V protested
  4. 1835: english as a medium of instruction in schools
  5. 1854: Wood's Dispatch, to educate masses
