CDM vs Non cooperation movement
  1. this time objective was of complete independence
  2. method used was violation of law and not just non cooperation
  3. this time less participation of lawyers and other class, unline in non cooperation lawyers gave up practice and students left schools
  4. muslims were very less this time, in non cooperation movement muslims were with congress due to khilafat issue
  5. no major labour upsurge
  6. more arrests this time
  7. congress was stronger this time

Communal Award and Poona Pact
  1.  announced by ramsay macdonald 1932
  2. based on findings of Lothian Committee
    1. separate electorates and reserved seats for minorities
    2. including depressed class 
    3. muslims, europeans, sikhs, indian, depressed class and even marathas in bombay
    4. ambedkar stressed on separate electorate for depressed class and to consider them as minority
    5. note: ambedkar had requested simon commission for above but in its report commission rejected the demand
    6. again in period of RTCs ambedkar reiterated his demands but gandhi opposed and said he is sole representative of all the people of india
    7. gandhi even agreed to strike a deal with muslims as long as they opposed idea of separate electorate
  3. provisions of communal
    1. seats on communal basis
    2. muslims where in minority gets advantage
    3. 3% reservation for women
    4. depressed class declared minority
    5. they also got double vote 1 for general 1 for separate electorate 
    6. even marathas got reservation in bombay
  4. congress though opposed but did not in favour of changing without consent of minorities. congress decided neither to accept nor reject
  5. Gandhi's drama on awards
    1. gandhi saw right through the game played by british
    2. he argued that with separate electorate for depressed class, only political representation is secured not their status
    3. they will continue to live as untouchables in society
    4. so he went on fast unto death in 1932
  6. Poona pact
    1. ambedkar abandoned the idea of separate electorate and instead reservation was extended
    2. govt. accepted and amended communal awards

  2. set up All India anti untouchability league 1932

  7. gandhi never mixed removal of untouchability and inter caste marriage or dinning
  8. untouchability and caste system are different
  9. abolition of untouchability is different from removal of caste system
  10. with no untouchability castes can exist complementary to each other not as higher or lower caste
  11. but ambedkar said annihilation of caste system to remove untouchability
