Anglo Mysore Wars Total 4 wars:1769;1799


First Anglo Mysore War (1766-1769)
  1. EIC vs Hyder Ali
  2. Time:1766-1769
  3. Result: Treaty of Madras 1769 in favour of Hyder
  4. Background:
  5. Hyder Ali built a strong army and annexed many regions in the South including Bidnur, Canara, Sera, Malabar and Sunda
  6. he also defeated large armies of EIC, took French support
  7. British formed alliance with Marathas, Nizam of Hyderabad
  8. Hyder Ali was able to bring the Marathas and the Nizam to his side with skilful diplomacy
  9. But the British under General Smith defeated Ali in 1767
  10. His son Tipu Sultan advanced towards Madras against the English
  11. At the Treaty in which mutual help from attack, but EIC never honored the Treaty.

Second Anglo-Mysore War (1780 – 1784)
  1. Hasting vs Hyder
  2. Time: 1780-1784
  3. Result: Treaty of Mangalore 1784
  4. Background:
  5. 1771: Marathas attacked Mysore, But the British refused to honour the Treaty of Madras and did not give support to Hyder Ali.
  6. As a result, Hyder Ali’s territories were taken by the Marathas
  7. had to buy peace with the Marathas for a sum of Rs.36 lakh and another annual tribute
  8. Hyder Ali who started hating the British
  9. English attacked Mahe, a French possession under Hyder Ali’s dominion, he declared war on the English in 1780
  10. This time Hyder Ali formed alliance with Nizam and the Marathas and defeated the British forces in Arcot
  11. Hyder Ali died in 1782 and the war was continued by his son Tipu Sultan
  12. Eyre Coote under Warren Hasting, who had earlier defeated Hyder Ali, ali died in 1782
  13. Both sides did not win and signed treaty

Third Anglo-Mysore War (1786 – 1792)
  1. Cornwallis vs Tipu
  2. Time 1786-1792
  3. Result: Treaty of Seringapatam 1792
  4. The British started improving their relationship with the Nizam of Hyderabad and the Marathas.
  5. Tipu Sultan, who assumed control of Mysore after Hyder Ali’s death, had French help in bettering his military resources
  6. He also refused to free the English prisoners taken during the second Anglo-Mysore war as per the Treaty of Mangalore
  7. Tipu declared war on Travancore in 1789. Travancore was a friendly state of the British
  8. 1790 Cornwallis declared war on Tipu
  9. Tipu was defeated in the first phase of the war and his forces had to retreat
  10. Later the English advanced towards Tipu’s capital of Seringapatam and Tipu had to bargain for peace
  11. Tipu had to cede half of his kingdom to the English including the areas of Malabar, Dindigul, Coorg and Baramahal
  12. Paid Rs.3 Cr as war indemnity to the British
  13. Surrender two of his sons as surety to the British till he paid his due

Fourth Anglo-Mysore War (1799)
  1. Wellesley vs Tipu
  2. Time: 1799
  3. Result: Tipu died
  4. Background:
  5. Treaty of Seringapatam failed to bring peace between Tipu and the English
  6. Tipu also refused to accept the Subsidiary Alliance of Lord Wellesley
  7. Tipu aligned with the French which the British saw as a threat
  8. Mysore was attacked from all four sides
  9. The Marathas and the Nizam invaded from the North
  10. Tipu’s troops were outnumbered 4:1
  11. British secured a decisive victory at the Battle of Seringapatam in 1799
  12. Tipu died while defending the city
  13. Tipu’s territories were divided between the British and the Nizam of Hyderabad
  14. Area around Seringapatam and Mysore was restored to the Wodeyar dynasty who had been ruling Mysore before Hyder Ali became the de-facto ruler
  15. Mysore entered into a Subsidiary Alliance with the British and a British resident was placed at the Mysore Court
  16. The Kingdom of Mysore remained a princely state not directly under the British until 1947 when it chose to join the Indian Union.
