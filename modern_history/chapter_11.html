Chapter 11 Economic impact of British Rule

Ruin of Artisans and Craftsmen
Sudden and Quick collapse of urban handicrafts
due to large competition by cheaper imported machine made goods form Britain
After 1813 British focus on one way free trade
Indian cotton textiles could not compete with goods made by steam-operated machines
Ruin of industries, Rural artisans more rapidly after the railways
Cotton weaving, spinning industries worst hit
Gradual disappearance of rulers and kings affected the market as they were main customers
Now, British officers in place of rulers, they patronize their own home made products
Policies were made to export raw material from India to England

No modernization of Indian market, unemployment increases
Workers were forced to crown into agriculture, added general pressure on land
Self-sufficient villages got destroyed

Zamindari, Mhalwari, Ryotwati all oppressed peasants no matter what the system was.
Royt and Mhalwari landlords were replaced by govt. and levied excessive land revenue.


Land revenue has to be paid on fixed dates.
