COMPANY RULE 1773-1853

1.     REGULATING ACT 1773: Gov.-Gen(Bengal), Executive Council to Assist, Madras & Bombay subordinate to Bengal, SC(1774), Bribes + Trade banned

1.      Governor of Bengal > Governor-General of Bengal Hastings

2.     Executive Council of four members to assist him.

3.     Bombay and Madras Subordinate to Bengal

4.     Supreme Court at Calcutta (1774), 1 chief justice + 3 other judges.

5.     Private trade, bribes, accepting presents banned.

6.     Court of Directors >report on its Revenue, Civil, and Military Affairs

2.    Pitts ACT 1784: COD(commercial)+BOC(political), Territories: British Possessions

1.      Gov.-Gen: Cornwallis

2.     Commercial and Political functions separate.

a)     Court of Directors>Commercial Affairs

b)    Board of Control>Real Control, Political Affairs

c)    Double Government system

3.     Board of Control >Civil, Military, Revenues, Real control by Govt. of England over EIC.

4.     Companys territories in India were for the first time called the British possessions in India;

5.     British Government got supreme control

 

3.    Charter ACT 1813

       Trade Monopoly of EIC ended

 

4.     Charter ACT 1833: EIC Pure Admin., Madras & Bombay no legislative powers, Previous: Regulations, Present: Acts

1.      Governor-General of India: Bentick

2.     Bombay and Madras deprived of legislative powers

                                                    i.     Laws made under the previous acts >Regulations

                                                   ii.     Laws made under this acts>Acts.

3.     EIC no longer Commercial body, ✓Purely Administrative body.

4.     Attempted to introduce a system of Open Competition for selection of civil servants, and stated that the Indians should not be debarred from holding any place, office and employment under the Company. However, this provision was negated after opposition from the Court of Directors.

5.    Charter ACT 1853: ILC formed, New Members(4 Local), Open Civil Competition, Macaulay Committee(1854)

1.      Gov.-Gen: Dalhousie

2.     Formed Legislative Council > Indian Legislative Council> later laws came as Indian council Acts.

3.     Separate Legislative and Executive functions of council, council functioned as mini-parliament on make policies and laws

4.     Six new members to Legislative Councillors to the council.

5.     Local representation in the Indian (Central) Legislative Council.

6.     4/6 new members, from local (provincial) governments of Madras, Bombay, Bengal and Agra

7.     Open competition system of selection and recruitment of civil servants to all.

8.     Macaulay Committee(1854) (the Committee on the Indian Civil Service) 1854

9.     Extended the Companys rule But, it did not specify any particular period

 

THE CROWN RULE (18581947)

6.     Government of India ACT 1858: Secretary of State: 15 member council, Viceroy: Canning, Double Govt. ended BOC+COD,

1.      Governor-General > Viceroy: Canning

2.     Double Government Ended >started by Pitts ACT 1784 (BOC+COD)

3.     New Office Secretary of State for India >Complete authority and control over Indian administration.

4.     15-member Council Advisory nature assists Secretary of State.

 

7.    Indian Council ACT 1861: Decentralisation: 3 Indians in council, Bombay & Madras got legislative powers, Canning Portfolio system Viceroy Ordinances power,

1.      Non-official members to Council

                                                    i.     1862, Lord Canning > Raja of Banaras, the Maharaja of Patiala and Sir Dinkar Rao.

2.     Decentralisation started:

3.     Legislative powers of Bombay and Madras were RESTORED(taken by Charter ACT 1833)

4.     Establishment of new legislative councils for Bengal, North-Western Frontier Province (NWFP) and Punjab, which were established in 1862, 1866 and 1897 respectively.

5.     PORTFOLIO system started by CANNING in 1859

6.     Viceroy can issue ordinances, without the concurrence of the legislative council, during an emergency. The life of such an ordinance was six months.

8.    Indian Council ACT 1892: More non-official members, Nomination: election type, Council can discuss budget

       More (non-official) members in the Central and provincial legislative councils, but maintained the official majority

       Legislative councils got power of discussing the budget, addressing questions to the executive.

       Nomination of some non-official members of the (a) Central Legislative Council by the viceroy on the recommendation of the provincial legislative councils and the Bengal Chamber of Commerce, and (b) that of the Provincial legislative councils by the Governors on the recommendation of the district boards, municipalities, universities, trade associations, zamindars and chambers.

       Limited and Indirect provision for the use of election in filling up some of the non-official seats both in the Central and provincial legislative councils. The word election was, however, not used in the act. The process was described as nomination made on the recommendation of certain bodies

 

9.    Indian Council ACT 1909

       Morley-Minto Reforms (Morley: Secretary, Minto: Viceroy

       Increased the size of councils: in Centre from 16 to 60, provincial not uniform.

       Official majority in the Central, Provincial Non-official majority

       Councils got more powers, budget, ask questions etc.

       Indians in executive Councils of the Viceroy: Satyendra Prasad Sinha: Law member.

       Communal Representation for Muslims: Separate Electorate.

       Lord Minto: Father of Communal Electorate.

       Separate representation of presidency corporations, chambers of commerce, universities and zamindars.

10.  Government of Indian ACT 1919

       August 20, 1917, Government declared: Introduction of responsible government in India

       Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms (Montagu: Secretary, Chelmsford: Viceroy)

       Central and provincial allowed making laws respective list of subjects.

       Provincial subjects into two parts

  Transferred : RESPONSIBLE to Council

  Reserved: WITHOUT RESPONSIBLE to Council.

       DYARCHY: Double Rule, Unsuccessful

       BICAMERALISM AND DIRECT ELECTIONS in the country

  Upper House (Council of State)

  Lower House (Legislative Assembly).

  Chosen by Direct Election.

       4. 3/6 were to be INDIANS in Viceroys executive Council

       Extended Communal Representation

  Sikhs, Indian Christians, Anglo-Indians and Europeans

       Franchise to a limited number of people on the basis of property, tax or education

       New office of the High Commissioner for India in London and transferred some of the functions hitherto performed by the Secretary of State for India

       Establishment of a public service commission> Central Public Service Commission set up in 1926

       Provincial Budgets Separate from the Central budget and authorised the provincial legislatures to enact their budgets

       Appointment of a statutory commission to inquire into and report on its working after ten years of its coming into force

12. Government of India Act of 1935

       Second milestone towards a completely responsible government in India

       Document having 321 Sections and 10 Schedules.

       All-India Federation establishment

  Three lists

       Federal List (for Centre, with 59 items)

       Provincial List (for provinces, with 54 items)

       Concurrent List (for both, with 36 items)

  Residuary powers: Viceroy.

       Abolished DYARCHY (started in 1919) in provinces but continued at Centre

       Provincial Autonomy.

       Introduced responsible governments: Governor to Act with the advice of ministers> came into effect in 1937 and was discontinued in 1939.

       Federal subjects: RESERVED & TRANSFERRED subjects. However, this provision of the Act did not come into operation at all.

       Bicameralism in six out of eleven provinces > Bengal, Bombay, Madras, Bihar, Assam and the United Provinces were made bicameral consisting of a legislative council (upper house) and a legislative assembly (lower house).

       Communal representation extended to:

  Depressed Classes (scheduled castes), women and labour (workers)

       Abolished the Council of India, established by the Government of India Act of 1858.

  The secretary of state for India was provided with a team of advisors

       Extended Franchise. About 10 per cent of the total population got the voting right

       Reserve Bank of India to control the currency and credit of the country

       Establishment of

  Federal Public Service Commission

  Provincial Public Service Commission

  Joint Public Service Commission for two or more provinces.

       Establishment Federal Court, which was set up in 1937.

13. Indian Independence Act of 1947

       February 20, 1947, Atlee declared that the British rule in India would end by June 30, 1948; after which the power would be transferred to responsible Indian hands.

       Agitation by the Muslim League demanding partition of the country

       June 3, 1947, the British Government made it clear that any Constitution framed by the Constituent Assembly of India (formed in 1946) cannot apply to those parts of the country which were unwilling to accept it.

       On the same day (June 3, 1947), Lord Mountbatten, the viceroy of India, put forth the partition plan, known as the Mountbatten Plan.

  The plan was accepted by the Congress and the Muslim League.

       Features

       Ended the British rule in India and declared India as an independent and sovereign state from August 15, 1947P

       Provided for the partition of India and creation of two independent dominions of India and Pakistan with the right to secede from the British Commonwealth

       Abolished the office of viceroy and provided, for each dominion, a governor-general, who was to be appointed by the British King on the advice of the dominion cabinet. His Majestys Government in Britain was to have no responsibility with respect to the Government of India or Pakistan

       Empowered Constituent Assemblies of the two dominions to frame and adopt any constitution for their respective nations and to repeal any act of the British Parliament, including the Independence act itself

       Empowered the Constituent Assemblies of both the dominions to legislate for their respective territories till the new constitutions were drafted and enforced. No Act of the British Parliament passed after August 15, 1947 was to extend to either of the new dominions unless it was extended thereto by a law of the legislature of the dominion.

       Abolished the Office of the Secretary of State

       States either to join the Dominion of India or Dominion of Pakistan or to remain independent

       Government of India Act of 1935, till the new Constitutions were framed. The dominions were however authorised to make modifications in the Act

       Deprived the British Monarch of his right to veto bills or ask for reservation of certain bills for his approval. But, this right was reserved for the Governor-General

       Governor-General would have full power to assent to any bill in the name of His Majesty

       It designated the Governor-General of India and the provincial governors as constitutional (nominal) heads of the states.

       They were made to act on the advice of the respective council of ministers in all mattersI

       Title of Emperor of India from the royal titles of the king of England dropped

       It discontinued the appointment to civil services and reservation of posts by the secretary of state for India. The members of the civil services appointed before August 15, 1947 would continue to enjoy all benefits that they were entitled to till that time.
