Chapter 10 Administrative Changes After 1858

GOI Act 1858 
1. EIC ended, rule in name of majesty
2. BOC and COD abolished
3. Gov-Gen. becomes Viceroy: Canning
4. Created Secretary of State with 4 member council to assist
1869 council became subordinate to Sect.
Complete control passess in hand of british parliament
1870 a submarine cable was laid down through Red Sea between England and India
Now, british parliament can control minute details of administrations
British parliament was shadowed by rich and powerful merchants to influence policy making

Indian Council Act 1861
1. gov. gen. council was increased
2. gov gen now could add 6 to 12 members in which half must be non-officials
Council had no real powers, only advisory in nature non binding on gov-gen
no control over budget
no control of financial matters
can't discuss actions of administration
no bill can become act unless approved by viceroy and on top of that by Sect, of State
Members were only nominated not elected
