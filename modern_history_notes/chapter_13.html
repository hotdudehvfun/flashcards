Chapter 13 Religious and Social Reform After 1858
Brahmo Samaj 1828
Location: Bengal
founder Raja Ram Mohan Roy
reforms in hindu Religions
worship one god
opposed idolatry, superstitious practices..
opposed caste, child marriage.
Teachings of vedas and upanishads
denial of priestly class
based on intellectual thinking of human
but confined to upper educated groups.

Reforms in Maharashtra
1840 Parmahans Mandali, opposed idolatry, cast system
Refromer: Gopal Hari Deshmukh, : Lokahitwadi

Prarthana Samaj by Atmaram Pandurang in 1867
reforming Hindu thoughts
worship of one god, opposed caste and priestly domination
Famous members: R.G Bhandarkar, Mahadev Govind Ranade
influenced by Brahmo Samaj


Arya Samaj(founded in 1875) and Swami Dayanand(1824-1883)
Hindu reform organisation in north india
bases on vedas, against priests, rituals etc
but total dependence on vedas gave an orthodox effect
favoured western science
Sunday meetings of arya samaj, similar to those of Brahmo, Prarthana samaj
also opposed conversion of hindu to other, later on this led to communism
somewhere led to divide people, instead of uniting them


Aligarh School(1875) founded by Sayyid Ahmad Khan (1817-1898)
Religious reforms were late in muslims, they always opposed western education,
remained uneducated, did not find place in government jobs.
Still, Muhammad Literary Society founded Calcutta 1863
promotion of religious ideas in light of western science
Ahmad Khan
Quran alone is authoritative work and other writings were secondary
founded Muhmad Anglo-Oriental College at Aligarh to spread western culture.
doors were open to all Indians
but later became communist, supported british govt. to save his education movement





Theosophical Society
founded in 1886 in USA by Madam H.P Blavatsky, Colonel H.S Olcott 
Annied Basant took leadership and came to india in 1893
Revial of ancient relgions of hinduism, zoroastrianism, buddhism
transmigration of the soul. unsuccessful
Annied Basant, Central Hindu School at Banaras later developed into BHU by madan mohan malaviya


Ramakrishna Mission(founded in 1896) and Vivekananda(1863-1902)
Ramakrinshna(1834-1886) saint guru of vivekananda
oneness of all religons, hindu reform, 
based on vedanta, rational thinking
he said isolation from other nations is cause of our degeneration
Mission: social work, humanitarian relief





Reforms in Parsis
Rehnumai Mazdayasan Sabha or Religious Reform Association
started by Naoroji Furdonji, Dadabhai Naoroji in 1851
against orthodoxy in parsis, education of women, position of women



Reforms in Sikhs
1920 Akali Movement purify sikh shrines
1912 satayagrah
1922 Sikh Gurudwara Act

Defects in Religious Reforms
1. Confined to upper and middle class only, neglected masses
2. Took false pride in greatness of past

social service and reforms. In the field of famine relief, union organisation, cooperatives and uplift of tribals and depressed
