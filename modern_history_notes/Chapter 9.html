Chapter 9 Administrative changes after 1858

GOI Act 1858
1. EIC ended
2. Gov. Gen became Viceroy: Canning
3. Secretary of State and his council created with council to aid him
4. Rule in the name of her majesty

1861 Indian Council Act
enlarged council
3 indians nominated
6 to 12 members nominated by gov-gen half at least non-officials member
these so called nominated members were big zamindars and rich people
council never had real control, real control by gov-gen and above that of secretary of state
council was a advisory body only
only function of council to give appearance of law having passed by them



1869 the council was completely subordinate to secretary
1870 submarine cable laid from London to India through Red Sea

Provincial Administration:
Three big presidencies: Bengal, Madras, Bombay
Ruled by Gov with council to aid

Rest were provinces:
they enjoyed greater autonomy
rules by LGs and Chief Commissioners

1870 Mayo took step to separate budgets of central and provincial

1882 Ripon : Local government

Changes in Army
Martial and non-martial class
martial: sikhs , gurkhas, pathans who helped britishers
non-martial: took part in revolt, awadh, bihar, central and south india
 
Indian Factory Act 1881
below age of 7 cannot work in factories, 7 to 9 age not more than 9 hours a day

Indian Factory Act 1891
weekly holiday

Above acts not applied to british owned tea and coffee plantations


1835 Charles Metcalfe Act
freed restrictions on press

1878 Vernacular Press Act
restrictions on press
people protested and act was repealed in 1882
again till swadeshi movement press enjoyed freedom but again after 1905 repressive press laws in 1908 and 1910
